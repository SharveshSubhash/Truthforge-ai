MAGISTRATES' COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. MC/CC 204/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v WONG SIU FONG

Date of Hearing: 6 February 2025
Coram: Magistrate Lim Pek Hoon
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Diana Soh Hwee Lin
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Thomas Yeo Chin Hwang

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused faces one charge of theft under s.379 of the Penal Code.
She is alleged to have stolen two bottles of premium whisky from
FairPrice Finest, Junction 8 Shopping Centre, Bishan, on 12 December 2024
at approximately 3:45pm. Total value of items: $178.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Joel Tay,
Loss Prevention Officer, FairPrice Finest Junction 8)

DPP SOH: Mr. Joel, what is your role at FairPrice Finest?
PW1: I am a loss prevention officer. I monitor the store for shoplifting
using CCTV and floor patrols.

DPP SOH: On 12 December 2024, what did you observe?
PW1: At approximately 3:41pm, I was monitoring Camera 7 which covers the
wines and spirits aisle. I observed a female subject, later identified
as Ms. Wong Siu Fong, selecting two bottles of Monkey Shoulder blended
Scotch whisky — these retail at $89 each. She placed both bottles into
a large canvas tote bag that she was carrying.

DPP SOH: Did she proceed to a cashier?
PW1: No. She walked directly from the spirits aisle to the produce section
at the back of the store, then walked to the exit. She did not approach
any of the three cashiers or self-checkout stations.

DPP SOH: What did you do?
PW1: I intercepted her at the exit at 3:46pm and asked her to accompany me
to the back office. She complied without incident. The two bottles of
Monkey Shoulder were found in her tote bag. Neither had been scanned
or paid for.

DPP SOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. YEO: Mr. Joel, is the CCTV footage in evidence?
PW1: Yes, Exhibit P1.

MR. YEO: The footage shows the accused taking two bottles. Did she at any
point walk towards the cashiers?
PW1: No. She did not approach any payment point.

MR. YEO: Was there any self-checkout machine near the exit?
PW1: There are self-checkout stations near the front of the store. She
passed them without stopping.

MR. YEO: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Ms. Hannah Ng
Bee Lan, Store Manager, FairPrice Finest Junction 8)

DPP SOH: Ms. Hannah, what happened after the accused was brought to the
back office?
PW2: I was called to the office. The two bottles of Monkey Shoulder —
retailing at $89 each, $178 total — were on the table. The accused's
tote bag was also there.

DPP SOH: What did the accused say when confronted?
PW2: She said she had intended to pay but forgot. She said she was in a
hurry. I asked her to show a receipt or any evidence of payment. She
could not.

DPP SOH: Was the FairPrice Plus membership card checked?
PW2: Yes. At the accused's request, we checked her FairPrice Plus
membership — card number XX-8842. The transaction history showed no
purchase from our store on 12 December 2024.

DPP SOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. YEO: Ms. Hannah, is it possible the accused could have paid by cash
at a cashier and the membership card was not used?
PW2: Technically yes, if she paid cash and did not scan her card. But the
CCTV clearly shows she did not approach any cashier.

MR. YEO: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW3 (Third Prosecution Witness — Corporal Serene
Goh Mei Ling, Singapore Police Force, Bishan Division)

DPP SOH: Corporal Goh, did you attend to the incident?
PW3: Yes. I was called to FairPrice Finest Junction 8 at 4:15pm on
12 December 2024. I took a statement from the accused and reviewed
the CCTV footage together with the loss prevention officer.

DPP SOH: Did the CCTV footage clearly show the accused taking the items?
PW3: Yes. The footage at 3:41pm clearly shows the accused removing two
bottles from the shelf and placing them inside her bag. She then proceeds
to the exit without stopping at any cashier. The timestamp and image
quality are clear.

DPP SOH: Was the accused cautioned?
PW3: Yes. I administered the standard caution under section 23 of the
Criminal Procedure Code. She was cautioned at 4:32pm.

DPP SOH: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Ms. Wong Siu Fong)

MR. YEO: Ms. Wong, did you intentionally steal the bottles?
DW1: No, Your Honour. I genuinely forgot to pay. I was preoccupied. My
mother is unwell and I had just received a message from the hospital.
I put the bottles in my bag because my hands were full. I intended to
pay. I simply forgot before leaving.

MR. YEO: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP SOH: Ms. Wong, you passed four cashier lanes and two self-checkout
stations without stopping. Can you explain?
DW1: I was not paying attention.

DPP SOH: The CCTV shows you looking directly at the self-checkout station
as you walked past it. You made eye contact with the cashier at Lane 2.
You did not stop.
DW1: I was distracted.

DPP SOH: You placed the bottles inside your bag in the aisle, not in a
shopping basket. Do you normally place items in your bag while shopping?
DW1: I do sometimes, yes, when my hands are full.

DPP SOH: There is no shopping basket in the footage. Did you take a basket
when you entered the store?
DW1: No, I intended to buy only a few things.

DPP SOH: No further questions.

COURT: All four witnesses are consistent on the following material facts:
(1) The accused removed two bottles from the shelf and placed them
in her bag at approximately 3:41pm; (2) She did not approach any
cashier or payment point; (3) The bottles were unpaid for. The
question of intent will be addressed in closing submissions.

[CASE ADJOURNED FOR SUBMISSIONS ON 12 FEBRUARY 2025]

DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 88/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v NANCY WONG MEI LAN

Date of Hearing: 12 February 2025
Coram: District Judge Sundaresh Menon Wei Liang
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Huang Xiu Li
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Francis Ong Chin Seng

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.323 read with s.73 of the Penal Code for
voluntarily causing hurt to her foreign domestic worker, Ms. Maria Santos
(Filipino national), on 15 August 2024 and 2 September 2024. The alleged
incidents took place at the accused's residence at Woodlands Drive 44.
The accused denies causing deliberate injury, claiming the worker's injuries
were self-inflicted or accidental.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Ms. Maria Santos,
victim, 31 years old, foreign domestic worker)

DPP HUANG: Ms. Maria, what happened on 15 August 2024?
PW1: Madam Nancy was angry because I scratched her kitchen cabinet
with a broom handle. She took a wooden ladle and hit me on my left
shoulder three times. It was very painful. Then on 2 September she
hit me again because I broke a plate. She hit me with her open hand —
slapped me on the left cheek, twice.

DPP HUANG: Did you seek medical attention?
PW1: On 16 August I went to the polyclinic at Woodlands. I told the
doctor I hurt my shoulder at work. I didn't say my employer hit me.

DPP HUANG: Why did you not tell the doctor the truth?
PW1: I was afraid of losing my job. Also afraid of Madam Nancy's
reaction. I made the police report only in October 2024 after my
work permit was not renewed and Madam Nancy was going to send me home.

DPP HUANG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. ONG: Ms. Maria, you made the police report after your work permit
was not renewed. Were you motivated by anger at Madam Nancy for not
renewing your permit?
PW1: I was hurt that she did not renew. But I was telling the truth.
The incidents really happened.

MR. ONG: In your police statement, Exhibit D3, when asked how many
times Madam Nancy hit you with the ladle, you said "two or three times."
Today in court you said "three times." Have you embellished the number?
PW1: It was three times. Maybe I said "two or three" because I was not
100% sure at the time of the statement.

MR. ONG: Your police report was made on 4 October 2024 — six weeks
after the second alleged incident. Why wait so long?
PW1: I was waiting to see if she would renew my permit. When she
gave me notice on 30 September, I went to the police.

MR. ONG: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Ms. Nancy Wong Mei Lan)

MR. ONG: Ms. Nancy, did you hit Ms. Maria with a wooden ladle?
DW1: Never. I have never used physical force on any of my helpers. I
have had three helpers over ten years. I have never been violent.

MR. ONG: What is your explanation for Ms. Maria's shoulder injury?
DW1: She told me she hurt her shoulder while moving heavy items in
the storeroom. I believed her. I even gave her money to go to the
polyclinic. She never mentioned anything about being hit.

MR. ONG: And the alleged slap on 2 September?
DW1: I raised my voice at her because she broke an expensive plate.
I did not slap her. I may have gestured with my hand — pointed at
the broken plate — but I did not touch her.

MR. ONG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP HUANG: Ms. Nancy, you said you have had "three helpers over ten
years." In your statement to MOM during the preliminary inquiry,
Exhibit P5, you said you have had "four helpers over eight years."
Which is correct?
DW1: Three over ten years is correct. I may have miscounted earlier.

DPP HUANG: You said you gave Ms. Maria money to go to the polyclinic.
Was this on 16 August 2024?
DW1: Yes, I believe so.

DPP HUANG: But in your police statement, Exhibit P6, you stated: "I
did not know she went to the polyclinic until the police told me." These
two accounts are contradictory. Either you gave her money for the
polyclinic, which means you knew about the injury, or you did not know
she went. Which is true?
DW1: I — I may have given her general household money that day. She may
have used it for the polyclinic. I did not specifically send her there.

DPP HUANG: When the police told you about the polyclinic visit, did
you say "I didn't know"?
DW1: I may have said that out of surprise. I don't recall exactly.

DPP HUANG: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following inconsistencies for consideration:
(1) The number of helpers over the years: the accused stated "three helpers
over ten years" in court but "four helpers over eight years" in her
MOM statement. This is a peripheral inconsistency.
(2) More significantly: the accused states she "did not know" the victim
went to the polyclinic (police statement) but also claims she "gave
her money to go to the polyclinic" (court testimony). These two
accounts are mutually exclusive. The court will need to resolve this.
(3) The timing and motivation of PW1's police report — made after her
work permit was not renewed — is a credibility factor to weigh.

[HEARING ADJOURNED FOR FURTHER EVIDENCE ON 17 FEBRUARY 2025]

DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 74/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v RAYMOND GOH CHEE SENG

Date of Hearing: 5 February 2025
Coram: District Judge Ho Shi Yuan
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Sandra Lim Hui Ying
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Bernard Wong Kok Wai

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.325 of the Penal Code for voluntarily causing
grievous hurt to his wife, Ms. Christine Tan Bee Lian, on 3 January 2025 at
their matrimonial home at Pasir Ris Drive 6. The victim sustained a fracture
to her left wrist and bruising to her ribs. The accused claims his wife's
injuries resulted from a fall.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Ms. Christine Tan
Bee Lian, victim, 38 years old, homemaker)

DPP LIM: Ms. Christine, what happened on 3 January 2025?
PW1: My husband Raymond came home late, around midnight. He had been
drinking. We argued about money. He became very angry. He grabbed my
left arm and twisted it violently. I screamed. He then punched me on
my right side — the ribs. I fell to the ground. He stopped when the
children woke up and cried. I took a taxi to Changi General Hospital
the next morning.

DPP LIM: Had there been previous incidents?
PW1: Yes. Three previous times in 2024. But I never reported because
I was afraid.

DPP LIM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. WONG: Ms. Christine, you said your husband twisted your left arm.
But in your police statement, Exhibit D2, you stated he "pushed me and
I fell against the sofa, hurting my arm." Which is correct?
PW1: He pushed me and also twisted my arm. I may not have included
all the details in my police statement because I was still frightened.

MR. WONG: You said he punched you on the right side. But your medical
report, Exhibit D4, shows bruising on the left lateral ribs. Which
side were you hit on?
PW1: I — may have been confused about left and right. It was the side
near the sofa arm.

MR. WONG: Your statement to the doctor at Changi General Hospital,
recorded in the A&E notes at Exhibit D5, states: "I fell down the stairs."
Can you explain why you told the doctor you fell down stairs?
PW1: I was scared. I did not want to cause trouble at the hospital. I
made up the story about the stairs.

MR. WONG: So you lied to the hospital staff?
PW1: I was frightened of Raymond. I only told the truth to the police.

MR. WONG: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Dr. Phyllis Ong
Xiu Ying, Emergency Medicine Consultant, Changi General Hospital)

DPP LIM: Dr. Phyllis, what were the victim's injuries?
PW2: Ms. Christine presented at 8:42am on 4 January 2025. She had a
distal radius fracture — a fracture at the wrist end of the radius bone
in her left forearm — and bruising over the left lateral rib cage,
consistent with impact. We assessed three to four ribs were bruised,
with no fractures. A wrist fracture of this type is commonly called
a "Colles' fracture."

DPP LIM: Are these injuries consistent with an assault as described
by the victim in court?
PW2: The wrist fracture is consistent with a forceful torsional
injury — twisting — or a fall onto an outstretched hand. The rib
bruising is more typically caused by direct impact, blunt force.

DPP LIM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. WONG: Dr. Phyllis, you said the wrist fracture is consistent with
either a twisting injury or a fall. Is a fall on stairs a plausible
mechanism for such injuries?
PW2: Yes. A person falling down stairs who puts out their hand to
break the fall could sustain this type of wrist fracture. And if
they impact a wall or banister on the way down, rib bruising is
also plausible.

MR. WONG: So the patient's original account — that she fell down
stairs — is medically consistent with her injuries?
PW2: Yes, mechanically consistent.

MR. WONG: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Raymond Goh Chee Seng)

MR. WONG: Mr. Raymond, what happened on 3 January 2025?
DW1: I came home at about 11:30pm. I admit I had some drinks. Christine
and I had an argument. She became very agitated and ran toward the
stairs. I tried to hold her back. She slipped on the top step and
fell partway down the stairs. I didn't assault her. She was hurt in
the fall and I was very worried.

MR. WONG: Why did you not take her to the hospital yourself?
DW1: She refused. She said she was okay. In the morning I was already
at work when she decided to go to the hospital.

MR. WONG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP LIM: Mr. Raymond, you said Christine "ran toward the stairs" and
you "tried to hold her back." If you were holding her back, your hands
would have been on her — specifically, her arm?
DW1: Yes, I grabbed her wrist area to stop her from running.

DPP LIM: So you admit to grabbing her wrist?
DW1: To prevent a fall, yes.

DPP LIM: The medical report indicates the fracture mechanism is
torsional — consistent with twisting. Simply grabbing a wrist to
prevent someone running would not normally cause a torsional Colles'
fracture. Did you twist her wrist?
DW1: I did not intentionally twist it. I was gripping her strongly
to pull her back.

DPP LIM: Your home does not have stairs. Exhibit P4 is the HDB floor
plan for your unit at Block 512, Pasir Ris Drive 6. It is a 4-room
HDB flat on the 10th floor. There is no internal staircase. How did
Christine fall down stairs?
DW1: I — I meant the corridor outside. She went out and fell in the
stairwell outside the flat.

DPP LIM: Your police statement from 6 January 2025 says the fall
happened "inside our home." Now you say the corridor stairwell. Which
is correct?
DW1: I was confused during the police statement.

DPP LIM: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following critical contradictions:
(1) The accused's claim that his wife fell down stairs is inconsistent
with the floor plan evidence: the matrimonial home is a standard HDB
flat without internal stairs, and the accused's own police statement
placed the fall "inside our home."
(2) The accused's account changed during cross-examination: from
"inside our home" to "the corridor stairwell."
(3) PW1's account in the A&E notes ("fell down stairs") differs from
her court testimony (twisting and punch), though PW1 offered an
explanation for this difference.
(4) The side of the rib bruising — PW1 says right side in court, but
medical records show left lateral bruising.

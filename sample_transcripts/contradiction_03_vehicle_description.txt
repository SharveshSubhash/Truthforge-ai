DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 45/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v MUHAMMAD HAFIZ BIN ZAINAL

Date of Hearing: 14 March 2025
Coram: District Judge Susan Koh Lay Hoon
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Mr. Ng Jia Wei
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Kelvin Lim Chong Peng

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.304A(b) of the Penal Code for causing death
by negligent driving. The deceased, Mr. Pradeep s/o Suppiah, was struck by a
vehicle on Tampines Avenue 10 near the junction with Tampines Street 83 on
18 November 2024 at approximately 11:45pm. He was pronounced dead at Changi
General Hospital at 12:54am on 19 November 2024. The prosecution alleges the
accused was driving a red Toyota Vios bearing licence plate SGP 8841J.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Alvin Chia Beng
Hock, eyewitness, was waiting at bus stop along Tampines Avenue 10)

DPP NG: Mr. Alvin, what did you see on the night of 18 November 2024?
PW1: I was at the bus stop on Tampines Avenue 10, just before the junction
with Street 83. A man was crossing the road at the pedestrian crossing.
The green man signal was showing. A car came from the direction of
Tampines MRT at very high speed and hit the pedestrian. The car did
not stop. It drove away towards Tampines Avenue 1.

DPP NG: Can you describe the car?
PW1: It was a saloon car. It was red. Under the streetlights it was clearly
red. I would say it was a Toyota — one of those small Toyotas.

DPP NG: Did you note the licence plate?
PW1: I tried to read it as the car sped away. I saw "SGP" and then "8841"
or something close. I'm not completely certain of the last number.

DPP NG: What time did this occur?
PW1: It was 11:43pm. I had just checked my phone because the last bus
usually comes at 11:45pm and I was worried I would miss it.

DPP NG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. LIM: Mr. Alvin, it was nearly midnight. How confident are you about
the colour of the car?
PW1: There are streetlights at that junction. The car was red. I'm sure.

MR. LIM: Is it possible the car was maroon or dark orange? At night, colours
can be difficult to distinguish.
PW1: It looked red to me.

MR. LIM: You said the licence plate had "SGP 8841 something." Could the
number have been 8814 or 8481?
PW1: Possibly. It happened very fast.

MR. LIM: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Ms. Rosnah bte
Mahmud, eyewitness, was walking along Tampines Avenue 10 from the opposite
direction)

DPP NG: Ms. Rosnah, did you witness the accident on 18 November 2024?
PW2: Yes. I was walking along the pavement on the other side of Tampines
Avenue 10 towards the MRT. I saw the car hit the man and drive off.

DPP NG: Can you describe the vehicle?
PW2: It was a dark-coloured car. I think it was dark blue or black. It was
moving very fast. The streetlights don't reach that well to my side
of the road.

DPP NG: Did you see the licence plate?
PW2: No, it was too far and too fast.

DPP NG: Did the car stop after the collision?
PW2: No. It just kept going.

DPP NG: What time did you observe this?
PW2: Around 11:50pm. I looked at my watch shortly after because I was
shocked and I remember thinking it was near midnight.

DPP NG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. LIM: Ms. Rosnah, you described the car as dark blue or black. PW1
described it as red. These descriptions are completely different.
Can you explain?
PW2: We were on opposite sides of the road. The lighting is different.
I can only say what I saw from my position.

MR. LIM: You also said the accident was at 11:50pm. PW1 said 11:43pm.
That is a 7-minute difference. Could you be mistaken?
PW2: I looked at my watch after the incident, not at the exact moment.
It may have taken me a minute or two to recover from the shock before
I checked the time.

MR. LIM: So the actual time of the accident could have been 11:47 or
11:48pm, when you account for the delay?
PW2: Possibly, yes.

MR. LIM: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW3 (Third Prosecution Witness — Inspector Fong
Kum Wah, Traffic Police, Investigating Officer)

DPP NG: Inspector Fong, what investigations were conducted?
PW3: We reviewed CCTV cameras along the route. We identified a red Toyota
Vios, licence plate SGP 8841J, captured on camera at the junction of
Tampines Avenue 1 and Tampines Link at 11:47pm travelling away from
the direction of the accident. This is approximately 4 minutes of driving
from the accident scene. This matched the partial plate given by PW1.

DPP NG: What further investigations confirmed the accused was driving?
PW3: The vehicle is registered to the accused's mother. We interviewed him
and he initially denied using the car that night. We obtained phone
records showing his mobile data was connected to a cell tower at
Tampines at 11:45pm on 18 November 2024. We also found a crack in
the front right headlight of the vehicle consistent with impact damage.
The accused subsequently admitted he was driving the vehicle but claimed
he did not realise he had hit anyone.

DPP NG: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (First Defence Witness — Mr. Muhammad Hafiz bin
Zainal, the Accused)

MR. LIM: Mr. Hafiz, were you driving your mother's car on the night of
18 November 2024?
DW1: Yes, I was.

MR. LIM: What route were you driving?
DW1: I was driving from Pasir Ris to Bedok. I took Tampines Avenue 10.

MR. LIM: Did you strike a pedestrian at the junction with Tampines Street 83?
DW1: No. I drove through that junction but I did not hit anyone. I heard
nothing. I felt nothing unusual. I did not know there was an accident
until the police came to my house the next morning.

MR. LIM: The headlight damage on the car — can you explain it?
DW1: I noticed the crack a few days before the incident. I had driven into
a bollard in a carpark. I kept meaning to fix it.

MR. LIM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP NG: Mr. Hafiz, PW1 saw a red saloon car strike the victim. Your mother's
Toyota Vios is red. You admit you were driving it along Tampines Avenue 10
at that time. CCTV captured your vehicle fleeing the area at 11:47pm.
Your phone was at Tampines at 11:45pm. Yet you say you hit nothing and
saw nothing?
DW1: I was driving normally. I did not feel any impact.

DPP NG: The victim weighs approximately 70 kilograms. Are you saying the
impact of a 70-kilogram person against your vehicle could not be felt?
DW1: I was listening to music. The volume was loud.

DPP NG: You initially denied using the car at all that night. You only
admitted to driving it after we confronted you with phone records.
Why did you lie?
DW1: I was scared. I didn't know what had happened. I panicked.

DPP NG: No further questions.

COURT: The court records that PW1 and PW2 gave materially contradictory
descriptions of the vehicle: PW1 describes a red car; PW2 describes
a dark blue or black car. The court will require the parties to address
this discrepancy. CCTV evidence shows a red Toyota Vios. The court also
notes the 7-minute discrepancy in the estimated time of the incident
between PW1 and PW2.

[HEARING ADJOURNED TO 17 MARCH 2025]

HIGH COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. HC/CC 8/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v MARCUS TAN KIAN HONG

Date of Hearing: 18 February 2025
Coram: Justice Chan Seng Onn
Counsel for Prosecution: Senior Deputy Public Prosecutor Mr. Victor Lim Wei Bin
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Isaac Tay SC and Ms. Cheryl Ng Xinyi

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused faces two charges under the Misuse of Drugs Act (Cap 185): one
charge under s.5(1)(a) for trafficking in not less than 28.63 grams of
diamorphine (heroin), and one charge under s.8(a) for possession of 2.4 grams
of methamphetamine. The accused denies trafficking, claiming the drugs found
in his car were planted by a CNB informant. The informant, PW3, was granted
a certificate of substantive assistance.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Staff Sergeant Lim
Cheng Hwee, Central Narcotics Bureau (CNB))

SDPP LIM: Staff Sergeant, can you describe the operation on 14 November 2024?
PW1: On 14 November 2024, I led a team of CNB officers to conduct
surveillance at the Geylang Lorong 14 carpark from 3pm. At 4:47pm
we observed the accused, Marcus Tan, enter the carpark in a silver
Toyota Camry bearing plate number SJH 8842 T. He parked in lot 18.
He exited the car and opened the boot. At that point we moved in to
arrest him. A search of the boot revealed two blocks wrapped in black
tape in a plastic bag. These were later confirmed by HSA to contain
heroin.

SDPP LIM: Was there an informant involved in this operation?
PW1: Yes. A registered informant, referred to in these proceedings as
"Source A," provided information on 13 November 2024 that the accused
would be collecting a consignment the following day.

SDPP LIM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. TAY SC: Staff Sergeant, when did you first receive the tip-off from
Source A?
PW1: At approximately 8:35pm on 13 November 2024.

MR. TAY SC: And Source A told you the accused would be at Geylang Lorong
14 carpark the next day?
PW1: Yes. At approximately 4:30 to 5pm.

MR. TAY SC: The accused's arrest record shows he was arrested at 4:51pm.
Yet the tip-off said "4:30 to 5pm." He arrived at 4:47pm. This is
remarkably precise. How would Source A know the exact time and location?
PW1: Informants often have inside information on drug transactions.

MR. TAY SC: No further questions at this time.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW3 (Third Prosecution Witness — referred to as
"Source A" per s.22 Misuse of Drugs Act non-disclosure; a person who provided
information to CNB)

[Note: Identity of PW3 is subject to a non-disclosure order. PW3 testified
in court under conditions protecting their identity.]

SDPP LIM: Source A, on 13 November 2024, did you receive information
about the accused?
PW3: Yes. A person I know contacted me and told me that Marcus Tan would
be collecting drugs from a contact the following afternoon at Geylang
Lorong 14 carpark. I reported this to my CNB handler the same evening.

SDPP LIM: Had you dealt directly with the accused before?
PW3: No. I received the information second-hand from an associate.

SDPP LIM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW3

MR. TAY SC: Source A, you say you received the information from an
"associate." What is this associate's relationship to the accused?
PW3: I cannot say without revealing my identity.

MR. TAY SC: In your statement recorded by CNB on 20 November 2024,
Exhibit D7, you stated — and I read from the statement — "I have
known Marcus Tan personally for two years." But today you say you
have not "dealt directly" with him. These are inconsistent — one
implies personal acquaintance, the other denies direct contact.
PW3: I know who he is. I have not personally transacted drugs with him.

MR. TAY SC: In a second statement, Exhibit D8, recorded on 5 January 2025,
you stated — and I quote — "I only knew of Marcus Tan through my
contact. I had never met him personally." Can you explain the
contradiction between "I have known Marcus Tan personally for two years"
in November and "I had never met him personally" in January?
PW3: I — the first statement may have been poorly worded. I meant I
knew of him by reputation.

MR. TAY SC: Did you at any time have access to the accused's vehicle?
PW3: No.

MR. TAY SC: On 13 November 2024 — the day before the arrest — where
were you between 10am and 4pm?
PW3: I was at home.

MR. TAY SC: Phone records in Exhibit D9 show a call between your
registered mobile number and a number registered to a person at
Geylang Lorong 14 at 2:17pm on 13 November 2024. Were you near the
accused's vehicle that afternoon?
PW3: I may have called someone in that area. I was not near the vehicle.

MR. TAY SC: No further questions.

---

COURT: The court notes the following significant inconsistencies in
PW3's testimony:
(1) PW3's first CNB statement (20 Nov 2024) states "I have known Marcus
Tan personally for two years" — which the witness now characterises as
knowing him "by reputation." This characterisation is not naturally
read from the words "personally" and "two years."
(2) PW3's second statement (5 Jan 2025) states "I had never met him
personally" — which directly contradicts the November statement.
(3) PW3's phone records place a call to the Geylang Lorong 14 area on
13 November 2024 — the day before the alleged drugs transaction — which
PW3 has not adequately explained.
(4) The Defence's planting theory requires further examination in light
of these inconsistencies.

DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 113/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v STEVEN ONG BENG KEAT

Date of Hearing: 11 March 2025
Coram: District Judge Patricia Chia Puay Leng
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Mr. Ivan Tay Chin Hong
Counsel for Defence: Ms. Nomvula Singh

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.323 of the Penal Code for voluntarily causing
hurt during an altercation outside Kovan MRT station at approximately 10:40pm
on 2 February 2025. The victim, Mr. Danny Foo Weng Keong, sustained bruising
to the left cheek and a laceration above the left eyebrow requiring three
stitches.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Danny Foo Weng
Keong, victim, 26 years old)

DPP TAY: Mr. Danny, what happened outside Kovan MRT on 2 February 2025?
PW1: I had just exited the MRT and was walking towards the bus interchange.
The accused came up to me — I believe it was a case of mistaken identity
initially. He accused me of bumping into his girlfriend earlier that
day at the station. I tried to explain I didn't know what he was talking
about. He was very agitated. Then he punched me twice on the left side
of my face. I fell against the railing and hit my head.

DPP TAY: Was there a girlfriend present?
PW1: Yes. She was standing slightly behind him. She tried to pull him
back but he shook her off.

DPP TAY: What was the accused wearing?
PW1: He was in a white T-shirt and dark pants. He was about 1.75 metres
tall. Chinese male, late 20s.

DPP TAY: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MS. SINGH: Mr. Danny, you said the accused "punched me twice." In your
police statement, Exhibit D1, you stated — and I quote — "He hit me
once very hard and I lost my footing." Now you say twice. Which is it?
PW1: He hit me — it could have been twice. It happened so fast. I
remember feeling two impacts but I may have been confused.

MS. SINGH: You said he accused you of "bumping into his girlfriend earlier
that day." In your police statement you said he accused you of
"harassing his girlfriend on the train." These are quite different
allegations. Can you clarify?
PW1: It was about an incident involving his girlfriend. The exact words
he used — I may not have remembered perfectly for the police statement.
He was angry about something involving her.

MS. SINGH: You said the girlfriend was "slightly behind him." In the
police statement you said she was "standing to his right, very close."
PW1: They were both nearby. I was focused on the accused.

MS. SINGH: Mr. Danny, the account you gave the police and the account you
have given this court differ on three specific details: the number of
punches, the nature of the allegation, and the girlfriend's position.
Are these discrepancies due to faulty memory?
PW1: It was a traumatic and fast-moving incident. The core facts are the
same — he punched me and I was injured.

MS. SINGH: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Ms. Priya
Ramasamy, bystander who witnessed the incident)

DPP TAY: Ms. Priya, did you see the altercation at Kovan MRT?
PW2: Yes. I was walking in the same direction as the victim. I saw two men
arguing. One man — the bigger one in a light-coloured top — suddenly
struck the other man in the face. The one who was hit stumbled back.

DPP TAY: Did you see how many times the victim was struck?
PW2: I saw one clear punch. There may have been another — it happened
quickly and I was still walking. I can only say for certain I saw
one punch land.

DPP TAY: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MS. SINGH: Ms. Priya, you said "one clear punch" and "there may have been
another." So you are not certain of the number of blows?
PW2: Only one was clear to me.

MS. SINGH: Did you see the accused's girlfriend?
PW2: There was a woman nearby. I thought she was a passerby at first.
She was standing a few metres away. She didn't seem to be involved
initially.

MS. SINGH: So she was a few metres away — not directly beside the accused?
PW2: That's how it looked from where I was.

MS. SINGH: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Steven Ong Beng Keat)

MS. SINGH: Mr. Steven, what is your account of events?
DW1: My girlfriend had messaged me saying a man had been staring at her
and making her uncomfortable at Kovan MRT earlier. I happened to be
in the area. When I came out of the MRT I saw a man I thought was
the person she described. I confronted him. I admit I may have been
too aggressive but I did not punch him. I pushed him — just once —
out of frustration. I didn't expect him to fall.

MS. SINGH: You pushed him, not punched him?
DW1: Yes. One push with my palm to his chest.

MS. SINGH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP TAY: Mr. Steven, the victim sustained a laceration above his eyebrow
requiring three stitches and bruising to the cheek. These are not
consistent with a single palm push to the chest. How do you explain
these injuries?
DW1: He stumbled and hit his face on the railing. The railing caused
the cut, not my hand.

DPP TAY: PW2 observed you strike the victim in the face. How do you
explain her evidence?
DW1: It was a push. From a distance it might look like a punch.

DPP TAY: You told the police in your first statement that you "didn't
touch him at all." Now you admit to a push. Your account has changed.
DW1: I was scared when I spoke to the police. I wasn't thinking clearly.
I later gave a supplementary statement admitting the push.

DPP TAY: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following inconsistencies for consideration:
(1) The victim described one punch in his police statement but two
punches in court; (2) The victim described the allegation differently
in court versus his police statement; (3) PW2 observed only one
clear blow; (4) The accused initially denied all contact, then
admitted to a push. These inconsistencies go to the quality and
reliability of the evidence but do not necessarily undermine the
core allegation that the accused caused hurt to the victim.

[HEARING ADJOURNED FOR FURTHER WITNESSES ON 13 MARCH 2025]

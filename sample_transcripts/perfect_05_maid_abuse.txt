DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 67/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v HELEN TAN HUI LING

Date of Hearing: 3 February 2025
Coram: District Judge Marcus Loh Teck Wee
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Angela Soh Pei Ling
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Mohan s/o Rajan

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.323 read with s.73 of the Penal Code for
voluntarily causing hurt to her foreign domestic worker, Ms. Siti Rahayu
binte Wahab (Indonesian national), over a period from March to September 2024
at the accused's residence at Tampines Avenue 7. Thirteen separate incidents
of assault are alleged. The accused pleaded guilty to six charges; the
remaining seven are for sentencing purposes.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Ms. Siti Rahayu
binte Wahab, victim, 27 years old, foreign domestic worker)

DPP SOH: Ms. Siti, can you tell the court generally what happened to you?
PW1: Yes. I worked for Madam Helen from 1 March 2024. From the first
week, she would slap me if I made mistakes — cooking, cleaning, ironing.
She slapped me hard, sometimes more than once. On three occasions she
used a clothes hanger to hit my arms and back. Once she poured hot water
on my hand — she said I was not washing dishes fast enough. I cried many
times but I was afraid to say anything because she told me she would
send me back to Indonesia and I would owe money to the agency.

DPP SOH: How were the incidents discovered?
PW1: In September 2024, I met a domestic worker from the same block
at the market. She saw the bruises on my arms and helped me contact
the Foreign Domestic Worker Association. They assisted me to make
a police report.

DPP SOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. MOHAN: Ms. Siti, you said you were afraid of being sent back to
Indonesia. Is it possible you are exaggerating the incidents to avoid
being repatriated and to secure a better employer?
PW1: No. I am telling the truth. I have bruises and a burn scar.

MR. MOHAN: Your police statement, Exhibit D2, lists incidents on specific
dates. How can you remember the dates so precisely after so many months?
PW1: I kept a diary in my phone. I wrote short notes after each incident
because I was afraid no one would believe me. Exhibit P8 is my phone
diary records, translated from Bahasa Indonesia.

MR. MOHAN: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Dr. James Tan
Zhong Yi, medical officer, Changi General Hospital)

DPP SOH: Dr. James, you examined the victim. What did you find?
PW2: I examined Ms. Siti on 15 September 2024. I found: multiple
contusions (bruises) in various stages of healing across both arms,
consistent with repeated blunt force trauma; linear contusions on
the upper back, consistent with being struck by a thin elongated
object such as a cane or hanger; and a healed burn scar on the right
palm, consistent with a contact burn from hot liquid or surface,
possibly 4 to 6 weeks old. The injuries were consistent with the
history of repeated physical abuse given by the patient.

DPP SOH: Were the injuries consistent with accidental causes?
PW2: The pattern — multiple contusions across both arms and back in
various stages of healing, combined with a contact burn — is not
consistent with accidental household injury. The combination and
distribution are highly consistent with repeated deliberate assault.

DPP SOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. MOHAN: Dr. James, can you exclude all accidental causes for any
single individual injury?
PW2: For any single injury in isolation, accidental cause could not
be excluded. But the pattern as a whole — number of injuries, distribution,
different ages of bruising, plus the burn — makes accidental cause
extremely unlikely.

MR. MOHAN: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW3 (Third Prosecution Witness — Ms. Farhana binte
Zulkifli, Welfare Officer, Foreign Domestic Worker Association for Social Support
(Fast))

DPP SOH: Ms. Farhana, when did you first contact the victim?
PW3: A domestic worker named Rosa contacted our hotline on 13 September
2024 and mentioned that her neighbour's domestic worker appeared injured.
I made contact with Ms. Siti on 14 September 2024 through Rosa. Ms. Siti
showed me her bruises. She appeared frightened and had difficulty speaking
initially. After building rapport, she described in detail the incidents
of abuse over the preceding months. Her account was consistent with
what she later gave to the police, and with what I had seen of her
physical condition.

DPP SOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW3

MR. MOHAN: Ms. Farhana, did you prompt Ms. Siti with leading questions
about abuse when you first met?
PW3: No. I asked open questions. I said: "Can you tell me how you are?"
and "Can you tell me what happened to you?" I did not suggest any
specific types of harm.

MR. MOHAN: No further questions.

COURT: The prosecution evidence in this matter is consistent, corroborated,
and mutually reinforcing:
- PW1 (victim): Detailed account corroborated by contemporaneous diary entries.
- PW2 (medical): Physical injuries consistent with repeated deliberate assault
  across multiple sessions, inconsistent with accidental causes.
- PW3 (welfare officer): Initial disclosure made to PW3 was consistent with
  subsequent police report and court testimony.
No material inconsistencies have been identified in the prosecution evidence.
The accused has pleaded guilty to six charges. Sentencing will proceed on
all thirteen charges.

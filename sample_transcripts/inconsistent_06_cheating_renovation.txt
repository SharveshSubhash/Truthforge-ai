DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 144/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v PETER LIM SWEE KEONG

Date of Hearing: 24 March 2025
Coram: District Judge Yang Shihui
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Rachel Teo Xin Yi
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Daniel Chua Eng Huat

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused, owner of PL Renovation Works, is charged under s.420 of the
Penal Code for cheating. It is alleged that between June and August 2024,
the accused induced four homeowners to make advance payments totalling
$89,400 for renovation works, then failed to carry out the works and
disappeared. The accused claims the non-completion was due to a subcontractor
failure and cost overruns, not dishonest intent.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Tony Chen Bak
Heng, complainant, 45 years old, engineer)

DPP TEO: Mr. Tony, what happened when you engaged PL Renovation Works?
PW1: I signed a contract with Mr. Peter Lim in June 2024 for a full
renovation of my 5-room flat at Sengkang. The contract price was $45,000.
I paid a 50% deposit of $22,500 on 15 June 2024.

DPP TEO: Was any work done?
PW1: Initially yes. Some hacking and tiling work started in July. But
by early August the workers stopped coming. Mr. Lim became uncontactable.
When I visited the site, the work was barely 20% complete.

DPP TEO: Have you recovered any money?
PW1: Nothing. I contacted the Consumer Association of Singapore. They
could not reach Mr. Lim either.

DPP TEO: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. CHUA: Mr. Tony, the contract, Exhibit D2, states payment terms of
50% on signing and 50% on completion. You paid 50% — $22,500 — as agreed?
PW1: Yes.

MR. CHUA: And some work was done — you said approximately 20% was
completed?
PW1: Some tiles were laid. But the quality was poor. The grout was uneven.

MR. CHUA: You said workers stopped in "early August." Was it the first
or second week?
PW1: Somewhere around the 5th to 10th of August. I'm not precise.

MR. CHUA: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Peter Lim Swee Keong)

MR. CHUA: Mr. Peter, what happened to the renovation jobs?
DW1: I had a subcontractor — Singapore Best Tile Works — who abandoned
all four jobs simultaneously in August 2024. They had financial difficulties.
All my materials were already paid for through them. I tried to find
replacement workers but the cost was too high. I ran out of money.

MR. CHUA: Did you inform the homeowners?
DW1: I sent WhatsApp messages to all of them in August. I explained the
situation and offered to refund after I sorted out a claim against the
subcontractor.

MR. CHUA: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP TEO: Mr. Peter, you said you "sent WhatsApp messages to all of them
in August." Mr. Tony's phone records, Exhibit P7, show no WhatsApp messages
from your number after 22 July 2024. Did you send messages to Mr. Tony?
DW1: I may have called him instead of WhatsApp. Or maybe I messaged from
a different number.

DPP TEO: What other number?
DW1: I changed my SIM card around that time. I may have messaged from
the new number.

DPP TEO: Your new number — what is it?
DW1: I don't remember it now. I have since changed again.

DPP TEO: Singapore Best Tile Works — did you file a civil claim against
them as you said you would?
DW1: I consulted a lawyer. But the cost of the claim was too high.

DPP TEO: You told PW1 you would "sort out a claim against the subcontractor."
Now you say you consulted a lawyer but found costs too high. Did you
ever tell the complainants you would not be pursuing the claim?
DW1: I — things were difficult. I was in financial stress.

DPP TEO: Singapore Best Tile Works' ACRA registration, Exhibit P9, shows
the company was struck off the register on 30 April 2024 — before your
contracts with the complainants were even signed in June 2024. How did
you hire a company that was already struck off?
DW1: I was not aware they were struck off. I dealt with them informally.

DPP TEO: You signed contracts with four homeowners knowing you had engaged
a subcontractor that was already dissolved?
DW1: I did not know.

DPP TEO: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following inconsistencies in the accused's
evidence:
(1) The accused claims to have informed the complainants via WhatsApp
in August 2024, but phone records show no such communication with
PW1 after 22 July 2024. The accused's explanation — a different SIM
card — is unverifiable.
(2) The accused's claim to have engaged "Singapore Best Tile Works"
as a subcontractor in June 2024 is undermined by ACRA records showing
that company was struck off in April 2024 — two months before the
contracts were signed.
(3) The accused's account of why works stopped evolved between his
police statement and his court testimony.

[HEARING ADJOURNED FOR FURTHER PROSECUTION WITNESSES ON 26 MARCH 2025]

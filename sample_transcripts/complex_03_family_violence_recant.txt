DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 204/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v MICHAEL TAN BOON LEONG

Date of Hearing: 28 April 2025
Coram: District Judge Noor Hafizah binte Rashid
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Goh Pei Shan
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Kumar s/o Arjunan

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.325 of the Penal Code for voluntarily causing
grievous hurt to his wife, Ms. Jennifer Lim Yoke Ling, on 14 December 2024.
The victim sustained two fractured ribs and bruising. The victim made a police
report on 15 December 2024 but subsequently provided a recantation statement
on 3 January 2025. The prosecution is proceeding with the original report
as a s.147 CPC application to use the original statement as evidence of truth.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Ms. Jennifer Lim
Yoke Ling, victim — testifying as adverse witness per s.156 CPC)

DPP GOH: Ms. Jennifer, on 15 December 2024, you made a police report.
What did you say happened?
PW1: I made a report but... I made a mistake. My husband did not
hurt me. I fell down the stairs. He was not even home.

DPP GOH: I refer you to Exhibit P2 — your police report dated 15 December.
It states: "My husband Michael hit me in the ribs repeatedly with a
wooden bat. I was crying and he continued until I could not breathe."
Did you make this report?
PW1: I... yes, but I was emotional. I exaggerated. He did push me and
I fell. That's all.

DPP GOH: Your Exhibit P3 is a video-recorded statement made on 17 December
to a police officer. In it you describe the attack in detail — you name
the weapon, you name the location in the flat, and you describe calling
for help from your daughter. Was that also an exaggeration?
PW1: [Pause] I was very emotional. I should not have said those things.

DPP GOH: Your Exhibit P3A is a medical report from Tan Tock Seng Hospital
showing two fractured ribs and contusions. You had fractured ribs.
Were those caused by a fall on stairs or by repeated blows with a bat?
PW1: [No answer]

DPP GOH: Did your husband threaten you or pressure you to change your
account?
PW1: No. I changed it because it was not accurate.

DPP GOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. KUMAR: Ms. Jennifer, you have clearly changed your account between
December and January. You are telling the truth today — that it was
a fall — correct?
PW1: Yes.

MR. KUMAR: Is it fair to say you were very distressed in December and
not thinking clearly?
PW1: Yes.

MR. KUMAR: And you and your husband have now reconciled?
PW1: We are working things out.

MR. KUMAR: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Dr. Kwok Pei
Shan, Emergency Medicine Specialist, Tan Tock Seng Hospital)

DPP GOH: Dr. Kwok, you examined the patient on 15 December 2024?
PW2: Yes. Ms. Jennifer presented with acute pain on the left chest wall.
X-rays confirmed fractures of the 6th and 7th ribs on the left side.
She also had contusions — bruises — in the same rib area and on her
left upper arm.

DPP GOH: Are these injuries consistent with a fall on stairs?
PW2: A stair fall could theoretically cause rib fractures if the person
impacts a hard edge. However, multiple rib fractures in the same lateral
zone combined with upper arm bruising would more commonly result from
repeated direct strikes. A fall on stairs typically causes more varied
injury distribution — hands, knees, face.

DPP GOH: What did the patient tell you about how she was injured?
PW2: She said, and I recorded in my notes: "My husband hit me with
something. I cannot breathe."

DPP GOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. KUMAR: Dr. Kwok, can you rule out a fall on stairs as the mechanism?
PW2: I cannot rule it out entirely. But the concentrated left-lateral
bruising pattern is more consistent with directed impact.

MR. KUMAR: Could the patient have been confused about the cause in the
emergency room, given her pain?
PW2: Patients in pain can be confused about some details. But the
statement "my husband hit me with something" is a fairly specific account.

MR. KUMAR: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Michael Tan Boon Leong)

MR. KUMAR: Mr. Michael, did you assault your wife?
DW1: No. I was not home. I was at a friend's house in Jurong until
about 2am. Jennifer fell on the stairs. When I got home at 2:30am she
was already hurt.

MR. KUMAR: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP GOH: Mr. Michael, your wife told the hospital — immediately after
the incident — that you hit her. She told the police the same. Her
video statement gives details only someone present would know. How do
you explain these detailed false allegations?
DW1: She was hysterical. We had argued on the phone that evening. She
was angry at me and blamed me.

DPP GOH: She identified the weapon as a "wooden bat." Does such an object
exist in your home?
DW1: We have a cricket bat that our son uses.

DPP GOH: Your daughter, who is 10 years old, gave a brief statement,
Exhibit P5, in which she says: "Daddy was hitting Mummy with a stick.
Mummy was crying." Did your daughter fabricate this also?
DW1: Children can be coached.

DPP GOH: By whom?
DW1: By whoever the police spoke to.

DPP GOH: No further questions.

COURT: This case presents the classic recantation scenario:
(1) PW1 made an initial detailed police report and video-recorded
statement alleging serious assault.
(2) PW1 subsequently provided a recantation attributed to distress
and reconciliation with the accused.
(3) Medical evidence — both the doctor's clinical observation and
PW1's own words at the hospital — is consistent with the original account.
(4) A child witness also provided a brief account corroborating the
original report.
(5) The accused's explanation that the daughter was "coached" is a
serious allegation requiring careful evaluation.
The court will apply s.147 CPC to determine the weight of the original
statements against the recantation.

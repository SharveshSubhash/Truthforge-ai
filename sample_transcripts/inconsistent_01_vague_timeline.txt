DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 99/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v GARY CHEN WEIMING

Date of Hearing: 5 March 2025
Coram: District Judge Mark Lim Teck Hian
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Fiona Koh Hui Shan
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Ramesh s/o Nair

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.506 of the Penal Code for criminal intimidation.
The complainant, Mr. Julius Pereira, alleges the accused followed him to his
home and made threatening remarks on the evening of 21 January 2025. The key
question is whether the accused was at the complainant's residence at the
material time.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Julius Pereira,
complainant, 29 years old, software engineer)

DPP KOH: Mr. Julius, what happened on the evening of 21 January 2025?
PW1: I was walking home from Toa Payoh MRT station. I had finished work
late. As I turned into my block at Lorong 7 Toa Payoh, I realised
Gary was following me. He came up to me at the foot of my block and
said — and I'm quoting — "I know where you live. You better watch
your back."

DPP KOH: Why would the accused threaten you?
PW1: We used to be business partners. We had a dispute over a business
that went badly. He blames me for the losses.

DPP KOH: What time did this incident occur?
PW1: It was in the evening. Sometime after 8pm. Maybe around 8:30pm.
I'm not completely sure of the exact time. I had been at the office
until quite late and I was tired.

DPP KOH: Did you report this to the police?
PW1: Yes. That same night. But it was much later — maybe around 11pm
by the time I calmed down and went to the police post.

DPP KOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. RAMESH: Mr. Julius, you said the incident was "around 8:30pm" but
you are "not completely sure." Could it have been as early as 7:30pm
or as late as 9:30pm?
PW1: It was definitely after dark. And I took the train — I usually
take the 7:55pm train from City Hall if I leave late. So it would
be at least 8:15pm by the time I got home.

MR. RAMESH: But you're not certain of the exact time?
PW1: No. But it was after 8pm, I'm confident of that.

MR. RAMESH: You filed your police report at 11pm. That is more than
two hours after the alleged incident. Why such a long delay?
PW1: I was shaken. I sat at home for a while before deciding to report it.

MR. RAMESH: Your police report, Exhibit D2, states the incident occurred
"at about 9pm." But you've told this court "around 8:30pm." Which is
correct?
PW1: Around that time. I may have said 9pm to the police because I wasn't
sure. It was somewhere between 8:30 and 9pm.

MR. RAMESH: So the time could be anywhere in a 30-minute window?
PW1: Yes. Roughly that window.

MR. RAMESH: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Ms. Goh Siew
Ching, neighbour of complainant, was at the void deck at the time)

DPP KOH: Ms. Goh, do you know Mr. Julius Pereira?
PW2: Yes, he lives in my block. I've seen him around.

DPP KOH: Did you see anything at the void deck on the evening of 21 January 2025?
PW2: Yes. I was at the void deck with my dog. I saw Julius come back
and there was another man who came up to him and spoke to him
aggressively. The other man pointed his finger at Julius and Julius
looked scared. The man then walked off towards Lorong 5.

DPP KOH: What time was this?
PW2: Hmm. It was in the evening. I usually take my dog out after dinner.
I eat dinner at about 7:30 or 8pm. So it could have been around
8:30 or 9pm. I wasn't wearing a watch.

DPP KOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. RAMESH: Ms. Goh, you said it was "around 8:30 or 9pm." That is
a 30-minute range. Do you have any way to be more precise?
PW2: Not really. I remember it was dark and the lamps in the void deck
were on. It was definitely evening, not late night.

MR. RAMESH: You said you take your dog out after dinner. Do you always
eat at 7:30 to 8pm?
PW2: More or less. Sometimes later if I'm busy.

MR. RAMESH: So the walk could have been at 9:30pm if you ate late that
night?
PW2: I don't think I ate that late that day, but I can't be certain.

MR. RAMESH: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Gary Chen Weiming)

MR. RAMESH: Mr. Gary, where were you on the evening of 21 January 2025?
DW1: I was at a function dinner at Grand Copthorne Waterfront Hotel.
It was a client dinner organised by my company. The dinner started
at 7pm and I left around 9:30pm.

MR. RAMESH: Can you produce evidence of your attendance?
DW1: Yes. I have the invitation, my name on the attendance list, and my
Grab receipt showing I was picked up from Grand Copthorne at 9:34pm.
[Exhibits D5, D6, D7 tendered.]

MR. RAMESH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP KOH: Mr. Gary, Grand Copthorne Waterfront is approximately 8
kilometres from Toa Payoh. The Grab receipt shows you left at 9:34pm.
Could you have left the dinner briefly between, say, 8pm and 8:45pm,
driven to Toa Payoh, and returned?
DW1: No. I was at the dinner throughout. I have colleagues who can confirm.

DPP KOH: You and Mr. Julius had a serious financial dispute. You have
motive to threaten him. Do you deny the bad blood between you?
DW1: We had a dispute, yes. But I did not threaten him. I was at the
hotel dinner.

DPP KOH: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes that while both PW1 and PW2 agree an incident
occurred at the void deck of Lorong 7 Toa Payoh, neither witness
can establish the precise time with certainty. PW1 estimates 8:30–9pm;
PW2 estimates 8:30–9pm. The accused's Grab receipt places him leaving
Grand Copthorne at 9:34pm. The critical question is whether the
incident occurred before or after 9:34pm, and this cannot be resolved
from the evidence currently before the court without further precision
on timing. Parties are directed to obtain phone records and hotel CCTV.

[HEARING ADJOURNED TO 10 MARCH 2025]

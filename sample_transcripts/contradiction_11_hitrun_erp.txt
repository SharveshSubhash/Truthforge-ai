DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 167/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v THOMAS WONG KAH SENG

Date of Hearing: 7 April 2025
Coram: District Judge Lim Jia Sheng
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Felicia Goh Kian Lin
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Rajan s/o Ramasamy

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.84 of the Road Traffic Act for failing to
render assistance after being involved in an accident causing injury, and
under s.65B for dangerous driving. On 22 February 2025 at 11:10pm, a vehicle
registered in the accused's name struck a pedestrian, Mr. Sahil Sharma, 29,
at the junction of Orchard Road and Paterson Road, and fled the scene.
Mr. Sharma suffered a broken leg. The accused claims he was not driving
his vehicle that night.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Sahil Sharma,
victim, 29 years old, software engineer)

DPP GOH: Mr. Sahil, what happened on 22 February 2025?
PW1: I was crossing Orchard Road at the pedestrian crossing with the
green man signal. A car came through the intersection and hit me on
my left side. I was thrown to the ground. The car slowed briefly
then accelerated away. I saw the rear of the car as it left — it was
a silver or grey sedan. I could not get the plate number.

DPP GOH: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Sergeant Chan
Boon Huat, Traffic Police, Investigation Officer)

DPP GOH: Sergeant Chan, how was the vehicle identified?
PW2: An ERP gantry at Orchard Road — the gantry at the junction of
Paterson Road — recorded a vehicle passing at 23:10:42 on 22 February 2025.
The vehicle registration was captured: SFG 4411 J. This vehicle is
registered to the accused. The LTA camera at the same junction captured
footage showing the vehicle running a red light and striking the victim.

DPP GOH: Is SFG 4411 J a silver sedan?
PW2: Yes. A silver Mitsubishi Attrage.

DPP GOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. RAJAN: Sergeant Chan, the ERP gantry records the vehicle's plate —
does it record the driver?
PW2: No. The gantry captures the plate number and timestamp only.

MR. RAJAN: So the ERP record confirms the vehicle was there, not
who was driving?
PW2: That is correct. The LTA camera may show the driver, depending
on resolution.

MR. RAJAN: What does the LTA camera show?
PW2: The driver appears to be a male. Facial features are not
sufficiently clear to make an identification from the camera alone.

MR. RAJAN: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Thomas Wong Kah Seng)

MR. RAJAN: Mr. Thomas, where were you on the night of 22 February 2025?
DW1: I was at home in Bukit Timah from about 9pm. My wife can confirm.
My car was not in my possession that night — I had lent it to my
younger brother, Alvin Wong, for the evening.

MR. RAJAN: When did your brother return the car?
DW1: He returned it the next morning, 23 February, at about 9am.

MR. RAJAN: Did you know about the accident when he returned the car?
DW1: No. He said nothing. I only found out when the police contacted me.

MR. RAJAN: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP GOH: Mr. Thomas, when the police first contacted you on 24 February 2025,
what did you say about the car?
DW1: I told them I was not driving.

DPP GOH: Your first police statement, Exhibit P5, records you saying:
"I was driving my car that night but I do not recall an accident." Why
did you tell the police on 24 February that you were driving — only to
now say you had lent the car to your brother?
DW1: I was confused during that interview. The officer was asking me
quickly. I corrected myself in my second statement.

DPP GOH: Your second statement, Exhibit P6, was recorded on 3 March 2025.
Between 24 February and 3 March, you say you "corrected yourself."
Is it not the case that you consulted your brother in that week?
DW1: I spoke to Alvin, yes. He admitted to me he had been in an accident.

DPP GOH: Your brother has not been charged. Your brother has not come
forward to the police. No statement has been recorded from him. If your
account is true, why has Alvin not handed himself in?
DW1: He is afraid.

DPP GOH: His EZ-Link card records, Exhibit P11, show multiple trips on
the MRT on the evening of 22 February — his phone pings from Tampines.
He was not in Orchard Road area.
DW1: He — he could have taken a taxi to Orchard after.

DPP GOH: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following serious contradictions:
(1) The accused told police on 24 February that he "was driving his car
that night." He now repudiates this and blames his brother. The original
admission is highly material.
(2) The brother (Alvin Wong) has not given a statement to police and
has not come forward, despite allegedly being responsible for a
hit-and-run injury.
(3) The brother's EZ-Link records appear inconsistent with him being in
the Orchard Road area that evening.
(4) The gap between the first and second statements — during which the
accused and his brother spoke — raises questions of fabrication.

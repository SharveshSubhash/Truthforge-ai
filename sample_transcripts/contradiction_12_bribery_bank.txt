DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 229/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v FRANCIS TAN CHENG HUAT

Date of Hearing: 12 May 2025
Coram: District Judge Ng Peng Hock
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Julia Lim Wei Min
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Tan Teck Hian SC

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused, a former Building and Construction Authority (BCA) inspector,
faces three charges under s.6(a) of the Prevention of Corruption Act (PCA)
for corruptly receiving gratification. It is alleged that between March
and July 2024, the accused received a total of S$18,500 in three payments
from Mr. Eric Wong, a building contractor, in exchange for favourable
inspection outcomes on three HDB upgrading projects. The accused claims
the money was legitimate loans from a friend.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Eric Wong
Bak Kuan, contractor, granted conditional immunity)

DPP LIM: Mr. Eric, why did you make payments to the accused?
PW1: Francis was the BCA inspector assigned to my three HDB projects.
In March 2024 he told me my first project had "issues" that would
require re-inspection and delay. He said it could be "resolved quickly"
with some "coffee money." I understood this as a bribe. I paid him
$5,000 cash. He passed the inspection the same week.

DPP LIM: Were there further payments?
PW1: Yes. S$7,000 in May 2024 and S$6,500 in July 2024, for the
second and third projects respectively. Total S$18,500.

DPP LIM: How did you make these payments?
PW1: All cash. I withdrew the amounts from my OCBC business account
before each meeting. The withdrawals are in my bank records.

DPP LIM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. TAN SC: Mr. Eric, you were also investigated by CPIB. You are
testifying under a conditional immunity arrangement?
PW1: Yes.

MR. TAN SC: If you do not cooperate truthfully, the immunity is revoked?
PW1: Yes.

MR. TAN SC: You therefore have every incentive to give the account
that satisfies the CPIB?
PW1: I am telling the truth.

MR. TAN SC: In your first CPIB statement, recorded 10 September 2024,
you stated the first payment was S$3,000, not S$5,000. In your second
statement on 15 October 2024, you revised this to S$5,000. Which is correct?
PW1: The S$5,000 is correct. I was nervous in the first interview and
I underestimated.

MR. TAN SC: Your bank records, Exhibit D5, show a cash withdrawal of
S$4,800 on 18 March 2024 — not S$5,000. If you paid S$5,000, where
did the extra $200 come from?
PW1: I may have had some cash on hand. I topped it up from pocket money.

MR. TAN SC: So the withdrawal amount does not precisely match the alleged
bribe amount?
PW1: Not precisely. But it is close.

MR. TAN SC: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Francis Tan Cheng Huat)

MR. TAN SC: Mr. Francis, what was the nature of your relationship with
Mr. Eric Wong?
DW1: We are friends from NS days — we served in the same unit 20 years
ago. We have kept in touch. He has occasionally lent me money when I
was in difficulty. The three payments in 2024 were loans.

MR. TAN SC: Did these loans relate to your professional decisions?
DW1: No. My inspection outcomes were based purely on merit. I have an
impeccable record — 15 years of service, no prior complaints.

MR. TAN SC: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP LIM: Mr. Francis, you say you and Eric Wong are old NS friends who
keep in touch. How often do you meet?
DW1: A few times a year.

DPP LIM: Your own phone records, Exhibit P9, show no communication
between your number and Mr. Wong's number in the 18 months prior to
March 2024 — when the alleged first bribe was made. How does this
reconcile with your claim of ongoing social contact?
DW1: We may have communicated through other channels. Or met socially
at group events.

DPP LIM: The timing: a payment in March 2024 coincides with the first
inspection; a payment in May coincides with the second; a payment in
July coincides with the third. Is this coincidence?
DW1: Yes. I needed money at those times. Eric was generous.

DPP LIM: Were there any loan agreements or acknowledgments of debt?
DW1: No. It was informal. Between friends.

DPP LIM: Have you repaid any of the S$18,500?
DW1: Not yet. I intend to.

DPP LIM: The three projects you inspected — all three passed on the
first re-inspection after the alleged payments. Did you note any
deficiencies in these projects?
DW1: My records show the works met standards.

DPP LIM: Your senior supervisor, Mr. Huang, testified in a voir dire
that two of these projects had known structural concerns flagged in
pre-inspection reports. Did you know of these concerns?
DW1: I relied on my own assessment on the day.

DPP LIM: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following contradictions:
(1) PW1's first CPIB statement alleged S$3,000 for the first payment;
he now says S$5,000. The bank withdrawal shows S$4,800.
(2) The accused claims a friendship with regular social contact, but
phone records show no direct communication for 18 months before the
alleged payments began.
(3) The timing of "loans" precisely matching inspection dates is a
significant circumstance to be weighed.

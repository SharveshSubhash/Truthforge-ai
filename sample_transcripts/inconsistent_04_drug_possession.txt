DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 102/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v DARREN ONG ZI YANG

Date of Hearing: 25 February 2025
Coram: District Judge Kevin Chan Wei Siang
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Mr. Nelson Tan Boon Seng
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Suresh s/o Pillai

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.8(a) of the Misuse of Drugs Act for possession
of 1.83 grams of cannabis. The drugs were found during a spot check at a
house party at a condominium unit in Jurong East on 2 February 2025. The
accused admits to being present at the party but claims the cannabis
belongs to another party-goer, name unknown. The issue before the court
is whether the accused knew of and had possession of the cannabis.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Corporal Hafiz bin
Sulaiman, Singapore Police Force, Jurong West Neighbourhood Police Post)

DPP TAN: Corporal Hafiz, what were the circumstances of the arrest?
PW1: On 2 February 2025 at approximately 1:40am, my team responded to
a noise complaint at Block 12, Jurong Gateway Road. On arriving at unit
#15-08, we were admitted by the occupant. There were approximately
12 persons inside. During a search, under the sofa cushion where the
accused was seated, I discovered a small zip-lock bag containing
a dried plant substance, subsequently confirmed by HSA as cannabis.

DPP TAN: Who was seated on the sofa?
PW1: The accused, Mr. Darren Ong. He was the only person seated on
that sofa at the time of the search.

DPP TAN: What was the accused's response when you found the cannabis?
PW1: He said, and I am recalling from my field notes: "That's not
mine. I don't know how it got there."

DPP TAN: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. SURESH: Corporal Hafiz, how many people were in the unit at the time?
PW1: Approximately 12 persons.

MR. SURESH: Were all 12 persons in the living room when you conducted
the search?
PW1: No. Some were in bedrooms. Approximately 6 to 7 were in the
living room area.

MR. SURESH: Did you observe how long the accused had been seated on
that particular sofa prior to your arrival?
PW1: No. We arrived after the door was opened. I cannot say how long
he had been there.

MR. SURESH: Could another party-goer have placed the bag under the
cushion and the accused simply sat there without knowing?
PW1: That is theoretically possible. I cannot rule it out.

MR. SURESH: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Darren Ong Zi Yang)

MR. SURESH: Mr. Darren, were you at the party on 2 February 2025?
DW1: Yes. I arrived at around 10:30pm.

MR. SURESH: Did the cannabis belong to you?
DW1: No. I do not take drugs. I don't even know what cannabis looks
like well. I was just sitting on the sofa talking to friends.

MR. SURESH: Do you know who the cannabis might have belonged to?
DW1: I have a suspicion but I cannot be 100% sure. During the party
I saw a guy — I don't know his name — he was sitting on that same
sofa earlier, maybe around midnight. He was rummaging through his
pocket a lot. He looked nervous. I think it may have been his.

MR. SURESH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP TAN: Mr. Darren, you said you arrived at "around 10:30pm." The
occupant's statement, Exhibit P6, says the party started at 9pm and
you were already there when the occupant arrived at 10pm. Which is
correct?
DW1: I may have arrived a bit earlier than 10:30. Maybe 10pm. I'm not
sure exactly. I wasn't checking the time.

DPP TAN: You said you saw "a guy" sitting on the sofa around midnight.
In your police statement on 3 February 2025, Exhibit P7, you said —
and I read — "I don't know whose drugs they are. I didn't see anyone
put anything there." You made no mention of a suspicious person at midnight.
Now you claim to have seen one. Why?
DW1: I was nervous during the police interview. I forgot about that
at the time. I remembered later.

DPP TAN: Can you describe this man?
DW1: Chinese male. Maybe mid-twenties. Wearing a white T-shirt.

DPP TAN: Of the 12 people at the party, how many were Chinese males
in their mid-twenties?
DW1: A few.

DPP TAN: Can you be more specific — how many?
DW1: Maybe four or five.

DPP TAN: So you cannot actually identify this person to the court.
DW1: No. I'm sorry.

DPP TAN: You also said you "don't know what cannabis looks like well."
Yet you were at a party with 12 people. Does this explain why you
did not notice a distinctive smell?
DW1: I — I didn't smell anything. There were other smells. Cigarette
smoke. Drinks.

DPP TAN: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following inconsistencies for consideration:
(1) The accused's account of his arrival time shifted from "around
10:30pm" in court to an acknowledgment it may have been as early as
10pm, inconsistent with the occupant's recollection that the accused
was already present at 10pm.
(2) The accused's police statement on 3 February 2025 made no mention
of a suspicious person at midnight — this narrative emerged only in
court testimony, five weeks later.
(3) The accused cannot identify the alleged alternative possessor
beyond "a Chinese male in a white T-shirt" among four or five such
persons at the party.
These inconsistencies go to the weight of the accused's exculpatory account.

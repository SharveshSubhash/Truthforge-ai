DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 131/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v KELVIN SIM YEW SENG

Date of Hearing: 17 March 2025
Coram: District Judge Chai Li Ling
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Mr. Malcolm Ng Wei Jie
Counsel for Defence: Ms. Shalini d/o Narayanan

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.454 of the Penal Code for housebreaking with
intent to commit theft. On 6 February 2025, a residential unit at Bishan
Street 22 was broken into and jewellery worth $8,400 and a laptop were stolen.
The accused's fingerprints were found on the kitchen window latch. The accused
claims he visited the unit legally the day before as a repairman.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Ms. Huang Fang,
resident, victim)

DPP NG: Ms. Huang, when did you discover the break-in?
PW1: On 6 February 2025, I returned home at about 6:30pm. My front door
was locked but when I went to the kitchen, the window was open. My bedroom
had been ransacked. My jewellery box was missing. My laptop was gone.
The kitchen window latch had been forced.

DPP NG: Had you authorised anyone to be in your home that day?
PW1: No. On 5 February — the day before — a repairman came to fix my
air-conditioning unit. He was sent by my managing agent, Comfort Property
Management. He only worked on the air-conditioning unit in the bedroom.
He had no reason to go near the kitchen window.

DPP NG: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Senior Station
Inspector Rajan s/o Krishnaswamy, Investigation Officer)

DPP NG: What did the forensic examination show?
PW2: A latent fingerprint was lifted from the kitchen window latch interior.
This was matched to the accused, Mr. Kelvin Sim, via the national fingerprint
database.

DPP NG: Did the managing agent's records confirm the accused carried out
the air-conditioning repair on 5 February?
PW2: Yes. Comfort Property Management's records show the accused attended
unit #09-44 on 5 February 2025. He signed in at 10:14am and signed out
at 11:47am.

DPP NG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MS. SHALINI: SSI Rajan, the air-conditioning unit in the bedroom — is it
located near the kitchen window?
PW2: No. The bedroom is at the opposite end of the flat from the kitchen.

MS. SHALINI: Were fingerprints found anywhere other than the kitchen
window latch?
PW2: The accused's fingerprints were also found on the bedroom air-conditioning
unit — which is explained by the repair visit.

MS. SHALINI: Were any other fingerprints found?
PW2: Several partial prints were found on various surfaces, none with
sufficient ridge detail to identify an individual other than the accused.

MS. SHALINI: Could a person conducting a thorough inspection of a flat
for other repair purposes have reason to touch a kitchen window latch?
PW2: Not if their stated scope of work was limited to air-conditioning.

MS. SHALINI: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Kelvin Sim Yew Seng)

MS. SHALINI: Mr. Kelvin, when you carried out the repair on 5 February,
did you touch the kitchen window?
DW1: Yes. When I arrived, I did a walkthrough of the flat to see if there
were other issues — loose fittings, water damage that might affect the
air-conditioning. I touched the kitchen window latch to check whether
it had a moisture seal issue. This is standard practice.

MS. SHALINI: Did Ms. Huang ask you to check for moisture issues?
DW1: She was not present. She had left a key with the property management.
I typically do a quick check of windows near air-conditioning ducts
because moisture from poor sealing can affect the units.

MS. SHALINI: Where were you on 6 February 2025?
DW1: I was on a job at Queenstown from 9am to 3pm. Then I went home.

MS. SHALINI: Can you prove this?
DW1: Yes. Comfort Property Management has my job sheet for 6 February —
I was at Block 48 Commonwealth Drive from 9:15am to 2:50pm. Exhibit D4.

MS. SHALINI: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP NG: Mr. Kelvin, you said you did a "walkthrough" of Ms. Huang's flat.
When Comfort Property Management sent you to that unit, what was your
written work order?
DW1: Air-conditioning maintenance — cleaning and filter replacement.

DPP NG: Your company's work orders, Exhibit P11, state: "Technician to
attend to specified unit only. No inspection of other units or common areas
without separate authorisation." Is a general walkthrough within your
authorised scope?
DW1: I use my judgment on the ground.

DPP NG: Do you have a record in your daily logs of checking the kitchen
window at Ms. Huang's unit?
DW1: No. It was a quick informal check.

DPP NG: You mentioned this "moisture check" practice for the first time
in your testimony here. In your police statement, Exhibit P12, when asked
why your fingerprints were on the kitchen window, you said: "I don't know.
Maybe I bumped it." Now you have a detailed explanation. When did you
develop this account?
DW1: I thought about it more carefully after the police interview.

DPP NG: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following significant inconsistency:
(1) When first asked about the fingerprint in his police statement, the
accused said "I don't know. Maybe I bumped it." In court he now offers
a detailed explanation about a "moisture seal inspection walkthrough."
(2) This explanation was not in the police statement and appears to have
emerged only in preparation for trial. The court will assess whether
this explanation is credible or a reconstruction.
(3) The alibi for 6 February 2025 is documented and will be verified.

HIGH COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. HC/CC 15/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v SIVA KUMAR s/o NARAYANAN

Date of Hearing: 25 March 2025
Coram: Justice Hoo Sheau Peng
Counsel for Prosecution: Senior Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Janice Ng Hui Lin
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Dhillon s/o Balwant Singh SC

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused faces a capital charge under s.5(1)(a) read with s.33(1) of the
Misuse of Drugs Act (Cap 185) for trafficking in not less than 33.44 grams
of diamorphine, which carries the mandatory death penalty unless the conditions
for the alternative sentencing regime are met. The accused claims he did not
know the plastic bag he collected contained diamorphine. This is Day 2 of trial.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Assistant
Superintendent Tan Wei Loong, CNB, officer in charge of the operation)

SDPP NG: ASP Tan, can you describe the operation on 22 September 2024?
PW1: On 22 September 2024, I led a team of 16 CNB officers in a
pre-planned operation based on intelligence received over the preceding
two weeks. At 3:42pm we placed the accused under surveillance from
the moment he arrived at Woodlands Checkpoint by bus from Malaysia.
We followed him as he took the MRT to Yishun Station, then walked
to Block 221, Yishun Avenue 9. He entered the ground-floor carpark
and retrieved a yellow plastic bag from behind a drain cover at lot 14.
At that point we arrested him. The bag was opened in his presence and
found to contain five bundles wrapped in newspaper.

SDPP NG: What were the bundles tested to contain?
PW1: The five bundles were submitted to HSA. They were confirmed to
contain not less than 33.44 grams of diamorphine in total.

SDPP NG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. DHILLON SC: ASP Tan, did the accused resist arrest or attempt to flee?
PW1: No. He complied immediately.

MR. DHILLON SC: He did not throw the bag away when he saw you?
PW1: No. He was holding the bag when we approached.

MR. DHILLON SC: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Dr. Lim Hui
Qing, Senior Analyst, Illicit Drugs Laboratory, Health Sciences Authority)

SDPP NG: Dr. Lim, you tested the contents of the five bundles?
PW2: Yes. Each bundle was individually opened and tested. I confirm
the findings as stated in my certificate of analysis, Exhibit P4:
Bundle 1 — 6.22 grams diamorphine; Bundle 2 — 7.04 grams; Bundle 3 —
6.89 grams; Bundle 4 — 6.55 grams; Bundle 5 — 6.74 grams. Total
diamorphine content: 33.44 grams. The threshold for the capital charge
under the MDA is 15 grams. The drugs in this case exceed that threshold
by more than double.

SDPP NG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. DHILLON SC: Dr. Lim, was each bundle individually weighed and tested?
PW2: Yes.

MR. DHILLON SC: The testing protocol and chain of custody — were these
followed for all five bundles?
PW2: Yes. The HSA's ISO-accredited protocol was followed. Chain of
custody is documented in Exhibit P4A.

MR. DHILLON SC: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW3 (Third Prosecution Witness — Corporal Ahmad
Faris bin Zulkifli, arresting officer)

SDPP NG: Corporal Faris, what did the accused say when arrested?
PW3: He was read his rights in English and Tamil. He was asked in Tamil:
"What is in the bag?" He said in Tamil — and this was recorded in my
field notes and confirmed by the Tamil interpreter: "I don't know. A
friend asked me to collect and deliver it."

SDPP NG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW3

MR. DHILLON SC: Corporal Faris, the accused said "a friend asked me to
collect and deliver it." Did he name the friend?
PW3: He said he did not know the friend's name. He referred to him as
"Ah Boy."

MR. DHILLON SC: Was that the full extent of his statement at arrest?
PW3: In the field, yes. A full cautioned statement was subsequently
recorded at CNB headquarters.

MR. DHILLON SC: No further questions.

COURT: The prosecution evidence in this case is consistent across all
three witnesses and corroborated by objective evidence:
- PW1 (arresting officer): Clear, detailed operational account;
  accused found in possession of the bag.
- PW2 (HSA analyst): Confirmed drug identity and weight through
  ISO-accredited procedure; chain of custody documented.
- PW3 (corporal): Accused's own words at arrest establish he knew
  he was collecting and delivering a consignment.
- No material inconsistencies are identified across the prosecution case.
The sole remaining issues are: (1) the accused's claimed lack of
knowledge of the drug type (which the presumption under s.18 MDA
addresses); and (2) eligibility for the alternative sentencing regime.

DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 53/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v RYAN TAN WEI HONG

Date of Hearing: 20 January 2025
Coram: District Judge Patricia Chia Puay Leng
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Mr. Danny Lau Beng Chye
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Eric Chua Wei Qiang

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.392 read with s.397 of the Penal Code for
robbery with hurt. It is alleged that on 5 December 2024 at approximately
2:15am, at a Shell petrol station at Upper Buona Vista Road, the accused
demanded cash from the cashier, Ms. Lily Wong, brandished a penknife,
and inflicted a laceration on Ms. Wong's forearm when she attempted to
trigger the alarm. The accused was apprehended at the scene.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Ms. Lily Wong Swee
Lian, victim, petrol station cashier)

DPP LAU: Ms. Lily, what happened at approximately 2:15am on 5 December 2024?
PW1: I was alone at the counter. A man came in. He walked up to the
counter and showed me a penknife. He said, very quietly, "Open the
till. Give me all the cash. Don't press anything." I was very scared.
I opened the till slowly, and as I did, I moved my elbow toward the
silent alarm button under the counter. He saw this and slashed my
forearm. I screamed. The alarm triggered. He panicked and ran toward
the forecourt but a lorry driver outside had seen everything and
tackled him to the ground.

DPP LAU: Did you get a clear look at him?
PW1: Yes. He was right in front of me, less than one metre, for about
two minutes. He was not wearing a mask. I could see his face clearly
under the bright station lights.

DPP LAU: Is he here in court?
PW1: Yes. The man at the dock.

DPP LAU: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. CHUA: Ms. Lily, at 2:15am, was the station well-lit?
PW1: Yes. Petrol stations are always brightly lit. The fluorescent lights
inside the kiosk are very bright, especially at night.

MR. CHUA: Do you have any doubt that the man who robbed you is the
accused?
PW1: None at all.

MR. CHUA: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Mr. Raman s/o
Krishnan, lorry driver, eyewitness)

DPP LAU: Mr. Raman, what did you observe?
PW2: I had pulled into the petrol station to refuel. While pumping
petrol I heard a scream from inside the kiosk. Through the glass window
I saw a man making a slashing motion at the cashier. I immediately
ran to the door. When the man ran out I tackled him to the ground. I
sat on him until the police arrived.

DPP LAU: Is the man you tackled here in court?
PW2: Yes. The accused.

DPP LAU: From the moment you heard the scream to the moment you had
the accused on the ground — how long?
PW2: Maybe 20 to 30 seconds. I moved quickly.

DPP LAU: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. CHUA: Mr. Raman, were you certain it was the cashier who screamed
and not someone else?
PW2: I saw the scream and the slashing motion simultaneously through
the window. It was the cashier.

MR. CHUA: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW3 (Third Prosecution Witness — Sergeant Azri bin
Mohd Yasin, First Responding Officer, Clementi Police Division)

DPP LAU: Sergeant Azri, what did you find on arrival?
PW3: We arrived at 2:23am. The accused was being held on the ground
by Mr. Raman. The accused had a penknife in his right jacket pocket —
confirmed by a body search. The blade of the penknife had what appeared
to be blood on it. The victim was being assisted inside, with a cloth
pressed to her forearm. She had a laceration of approximately 6cm.

DPP LAU: Was anyone else potentially responsible for the robbery?
PW3: No. The accused was the only individual present at the scene
aside from the victim and Mr. Raman. The CCTV footage, Exhibit P3,
confirms only one perpetrator entered the store.

DPP LAU: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW3

MR. CHUA: Sergeant Azri, was the accused wearing gloves?
PW3: No.

MR. CHUA: Was the accused's DNA found on the penknife?
PW3: The penknife was tested. DNA matching the accused was found on
the handle. The blood on the blade matched the victim's DNA profile.

MR. CHUA: My client does not contest being at the scene. He contests
that he intended to commit robbery — he claims he was there to confront
the cashier over a personal matter and the knife was for his own
protection. I have no further questions on the factual narrative.

COURT: The prosecution evidence in this case is consistent across all
witnesses and corroborated by objective evidence:
- PW1 (victim): Clear visual identification, direct account of robbery demand.
- PW2 (lorry driver): Independent witness who observed the robbery through
  the window and apprehended the accused.
- PW3 (police): Accused found at scene with weapon; DNA matches both
  accused and victim.
- CCTV (Exhibit P3): Confirms single perpetrator; timeline consistent with
  all witnesses.
The court notes no material inconsistencies in the prosecution's evidence.
The only issue remaining is the accused's intent, which is a legal question.

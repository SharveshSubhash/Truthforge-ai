DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 179/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v JASON QUEK BOON LIANG

Date of Hearing: 14 April 2025
Coram: District Judge Wong Heng Peng
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Siti Aishah binte Ibrahim
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Christopher Tan Wei Jian

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.323 of the Penal Code for voluntarily causing
hurt. It is alleged that on 1 March 2025 at approximately 12:45am, at a
bar on Club Street, the accused struck Mr. Dominic Yap Wei Sheng, causing
a split lip and chipped front tooth. The witnesses give partially inconsistent
accounts of who provoked whom and the sequence of events.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Dominic Yap
Wei Sheng, victim, 29 years old, marketing executive)

DPP AISHAH: Mr. Dominic, what happened at the bar?
PW1: I was at the bar with three friends celebrating a colleague's birthday.
Around 12:45am the accused bumped into me from behind and spilled my drink.
I said something like "Hey, watch it." He turned around and immediately
punched me in the face. I had done nothing to provoke him. He was
clearly drunk.

DPP AISHAH: Did you say anything beyond "hey, watch it"?
PW1: I may have said something like "what's wrong with you" as well.
But I did not touch him or threaten him.

DPP AISHAH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. TAN: Mr. Dominic, in your police statement on 1 March 2025, you said
"I turned and told him to watch himself." In court today you say you said
"hey, watch it" and also possibly "what's wrong with you." The words
are different. Did you use an aggressive tone?
PW1: I was surprised by the spill. I may have sounded annoyed.

MR. TAN: Your friend Mr. Justin Lim, who was with you, has given a
statement to the police, Exhibit D5, in which he says — and I read —
"Dominic grabbed the accused's shoulder and called him a foul name."
Did you do this?
PW1: I did not grab him. I may have touched his arm — a tap. And I
may have said something insulting. I was drunk too. But I did not
hit him first.

MR. TAN: So your friend Justin says you grabbed him and called him names —
and you now admit to possibly saying something insulting. Your account
has evolved during this cross-examination.
PW1: The core fact is he punched me.

MR. TAN: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Mr. Brendan Chua
Zi Long, bystander, was at adjacent table)

DPP AISHAH: Mr. Brendan, what did you observe?
PW2: I saw two men arguing. One of them — I think it was the bigger one —
threw a punch. The other one stumbled back. I saw the punch clearly.
There was blood from his mouth.

DPP AISHAH: Which man threw the punch?
PW2: The one I now know is the accused — he was standing and he threw
the punch at the other man.

DPP AISHAH: Did you see what happened before the punch?
PW2: I heard raised voices. I looked over when the argument got loud.
I did not see how it started.

DPP AISHAH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. TAN: Mr. Brendan, you said you heard raised voices and looked over —
so you did not see the beginning of the confrontation?
PW2: Correct.

MR. TAN: Is it possible the victim had pushed or grabbed the accused
before you started watching?
PW2: It is possible. I cannot say what happened before I looked.

MR. TAN: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Jason Quek Boon Liang)

MR. TAN: Mr. Jason, what happened?
DW1: I accidentally bumped into him. I was about to apologise when
he grabbed my collar with both hands and called me a vulgar Hokkien
word. I pushed his hands away. He then punched at me — I ducked and
my elbow caught his face accidentally. I did not intend to hurt him.

MR. TAN: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP AISHAH: Mr. Jason, you say "he punched at me and my elbow caught
his face accidentally." But in your police statement, Exhibit P8, you
said: "I pushed him away when he grabbed me. I did not punch him."
There is no mention of an elbow strike. Did you mention an elbow in
the police statement?
DW1: I did not describe it in detail at the time. The elbow happened
quickly.

DPP AISHAH: Your witness, Mr. Justin Lim — the victim's friend — actually
supports the account that the victim grabbed you. But PW2 saw you throw
a punch. Who is telling the truth — the victim's friend, your version,
or the independent witness?
DW1: Justin saw the grab. But he may have missed the elbow because it
was fast.

DPP AISHAH: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following inconsistencies:
(1) PW1's account of his own words escalated during cross-examination —
from "hey, watch it" to also "what's wrong with you" to possibly
insulting language and a touching of the arm.
(2) The victim's own friend (Mr. Justin Lim) says the victim grabbed
the accused's shoulder and called him a foul name — which somewhat
corroborates the accused's account of provocation.
(3) The accused's explanation changed: police statement says only "pushed
him away"; court testimony adds "accidental elbow" account.
(4) PW2 (independent witness) saw the accused throw a punch but did not
see the initial provocation.
The court must weigh whether the accused acted in self-defence or
retaliated disproportionately.

[HEARING ADJOURNED TO 16 APRIL 2025]

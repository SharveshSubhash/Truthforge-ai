SUPREME COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. HC/CC 12/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v RAJAN S/O KRISHNAMURTHY

Date of Hearing: 8 January 2025
Coram: Justice Valerie Thean
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Chua Shu Fen
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Mohamed Rizal bin Hamid

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.392 of the Penal Code for robbery at a 7-Eleven
convenience store at Block 231 Serangoon Avenue 2 on the night of 14 October 2024.
The charge states the robbery occurred at approximately 10:30pm.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Tan Boon Huat,
Store Cashier, 7-Eleven Block 231 Serangoon Avenue 2)

DPP CHUA: Mr. Tan, were you working at the 7-Eleven store on the night of
14 October 2024?
PW1: Yes, I was on the night shift that evening.

DPP CHUA: At what time did the incident occur?
PW1: It was at exactly 10:32pm. I remember because I had just finished
restocking the refrigerator and I looked at the clock on the wall. It
was 10:32pm when the man came in.

DPP CHUA: What happened when the man came in?
PW1: He came in wearing a grey hoodie and a surgical mask. He walked straight
to the counter and took out a knife — it looked like a kitchen knife —
and he demanded I open the cash register. He took about $340 in cash and
ran out through the main entrance.

DPP CHUA: Did you call the police immediately?
PW1: Yes. I called 999 right after he left. My call was made at 10:35pm
according to the records.

DPP CHUA: No further questions, Your Honour.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. RIZAL: Mr. Tan, you said you restocked the refrigerator before looking
at the clock. How long does restocking take?
PW1: About 20 to 25 minutes usually.

MR. RIZAL: And what time did you start restocking?
PW1: I think around 10pm or just after.

MR. RIZAL: So if you started at 10pm and restocking takes 25 minutes, you
could have finished as late as 10:25pm, correct?
PW1: I suppose so, yes.

MR. RIZAL: So the incident could have occurred at 10:25pm, not 10:32pm?
PW1: I'm certain I looked at the clock. It said 10:32pm.

MR. RIZAL: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Ms. Nadia bte Osman,
Customer, present at the store at time of incident)

DPP CHUA: Ms. Nadia, were you at the 7-Eleven store at Block 231 Serangoon
Avenue 2 on the night of 14 October 2024?
PW2: Yes, I had gone to buy some bottled water.

DPP CHUA: What time did you arrive at the store?
PW2: It was 10:28pm. I checked my phone just before I entered because I
had a message come in.

DPP CHUA: And how long were you inside before the robbery happened?
PW2: It happened almost immediately. I had barely picked up the bottle when
the man came in. Maybe one or two minutes after I entered.

DPP CHUA: So the robbery occurred at approximately 10:29 to 10:30pm?
PW2: Yes. Around 10:29pm or 10:30pm at the latest.

DPP CHUA: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. RIZAL: Ms. Nadia, you said you checked your phone before entering. Can
you show the court the message timestamp?
PW2: I have it here. [Exhibit P4 — Screenshot of WhatsApp message received
at 10:28pm on 14 October 2024 is shown to the court.]

MR. RIZAL: Thank you. No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW3 (Third Prosecution Witness — Senior Staff Sergeant
Fauziah bte Ahmad, Singapore Police Force, Ang Mo Kio Division)

DPP CHUA: SSgt Fauziah, what time did you receive the call about this incident?
PW3: The First Information Report was lodged at 10:35pm. My partner and I
arrived at the scene at 10:41pm.

DPP CHUA: And when you arrived, what did the store cashier tell you?
PW3: Mr. Tan told us the robbery had just taken place. He told us it happened
at 10:32pm.

DPP CHUA: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (First Defence Witness — Mr. Suresh s/o Pillai,
Neighbour of accused, Block 234 Serangoon Avenue 2)

MR. RIZAL: Mr. Suresh, do you know the accused, Mr. Rajan?
DW1: Yes, he is my neighbour. He lives on the same floor as me at Block 234.

MR. RIZAL: Were you with Mr. Rajan on the night of 14 October 2024?
DW1: Yes. We were watching football together at my flat. The English Premier
League match between Arsenal and Manchester City.

MR. RIZAL: What time did Mr. Rajan arrive at your flat?
DW1: He came at about 9:30pm. The match started at 9:00pm and he missed
the first half hour.

MR. RIZAL: And when did he leave?
DW1: He left at about 9:45pm to go downstairs to buy some drinks from the
7-Eleven. He came back maybe 20 minutes later, around 10:05pm.

MR. RIZAL: Did he return at 10:05pm and stay with you?
DW1: Yes. He stayed until the match ended at about 11:15pm. He never left
again after coming back at 10:05pm.

DPP CHUA: Mr. Suresh, you said Mr. Rajan left to go to the 7-Eleven at 9:45pm
and returned at 10:05pm. You are saying he left and came back before the
robbery occurred at 10:30pm — is that your evidence?
DW1: Yes. He was back home well before 10:30pm.

DPP CHUA: Are you certain of these times?
DW1: Well, I wasn't watching the clock constantly. I remember the half-time
whistle had just blown when he said he was going to the shop. Half-time
in that match was at 9:45pm. That's how I know.

DPP CHUA: Your Honour, I would draw the court's attention to Exhibit D3 —
the match schedule confirms half-time was at 9:47pm, not 9:45pm. No
further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW2 (Second Defence Witness — Mr. Rajan s/o
Krishnamurthy, the Accused)

MR. RIZAL: Mr. Rajan, did you commit the robbery at the 7-Eleven on
14 October 2024?
DW2: No, Your Honour. I did not.

MR. RIZAL: Where were you at 10:30pm on 14 October 2024?
DW2: I was at my neighbour Suresh's flat watching football. I had gone down
to the shop earlier, maybe around 9:45pm, and came back at around 10:10pm.
I was upstairs from 10:10pm onwards.

COURT: Mr. Rajan, you said you visited the 7-Eleven at 9:45pm. Is that the
same store at Block 231?
DW2: No, Your Honour. I went to the convenience store at Block 220. That's
the one nearer to the bus stop.

MR. RIZAL: No further questions.

DPP CHUA: Mr. Rajan, your neighbour Mr. Suresh said you went to the 7-Eleven,
but you say you went to a different store. Which is correct?
DW2: I just said "the shop." Suresh assumed it was 7-Eleven. I went to
Cheers at Block 220.

DPP CHUA: Do you have any receipt from Cheers at Block 220?
DW2: No, I paid cash.

DPP CHUA: No further questions.

---

COURT: The court notes the following material inconsistency for the record:
PW1 states the robbery occurred at 10:32pm; PW2, corroborated by a
timestamped phone message, places it at approximately 10:29-10:30pm.
DW1 states the accused returned from the shop at 10:05pm, while the
accused himself states he returned at 10:10pm. Parties are directed to
address these discrepancies in closing submissions.

[HEARING ADJOURNED TO 9 JANUARY 2025]

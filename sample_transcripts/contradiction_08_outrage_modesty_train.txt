DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 119/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v LOW CHIN SIONG

Date of Hearing: 6 March 2025
Coram: District Judge Tan Cheng Han
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Clara Ang Hwee Lin
Counsel for Defence: Mr. James Raj s/o Arumugam

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.354(1) of the Penal Code for outrage of modesty.
It is alleged that on 21 January 2025, on an SMRT North-South Line train
between Ang Mo Kio and Bishan stations, the accused used his hand to touch
the buttocks of the complainant, Ms. Ho Jia Ying. The accused denies the
allegation, claiming he was not on the train at the stated time.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Ms. Ho Jia Ying,
victim, 23 years old, university student)

DPP ANG: Ms. Ho, what happened on 21 January 2025?
PW1: I was taking the train from Ang Mo Kio to Bishan after dinner
with friends. The train was very crowded — it was around 9:15pm,
peak hour returning traffic. I was standing holding the pole. I felt
a hand touch my buttocks from behind. It was deliberate — not an
accidental brush. I turned around and the man behind me was looking
away. I took a photo of him on my phone discreetly. At Bishan I
alerted the SMRT staff.

DPP ANG: What was he wearing?
PW1: A dark green collared shirt. Short sleeves. Dark trousers.
He looked to be in his forties or fifties.

DPP ANG: Where exactly were you touched?
PW1: On the right side of my buttocks, from behind.

DPP ANG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. RAJ: Ms. Ho, in your police statement made on the same night,
Exhibit D2, you described the touch as being on your "left buttock."
Today you say "right side." Which is correct?
PW1: I was confused about directions. I was facing forward and he
was behind me. Looking back, it was my right side — which from his
perspective may have been on his left. I may have mixed up the
perspective.

MR. RAJ: The shirt — in your police statement you described "a dark
blue collared shirt." In court today you say "dark green." Which is correct?
PW1: I said dark — it was a dark colour. Under the train lighting it
could look blue or green. I'm not certain of the exact shade.

MR. RAJ: The photo you took on your phone — Exhibit P4 — shows a man
wearing what appears to be a dark shirt. Is this the accused?
PW1: Yes. That is the man.

MR. RAJ: The man in the photo is not in a collared shirt. He appears
to be wearing a T-shirt with a small design on the chest. Would you
agree?
PW1: Looking at the photo again... it may have been a T-shirt. I
thought it had a collar.

MR. RAJ: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Inspector Jenny
Tan Suan Li, Transport Police, SMRT)

DPP ANG: Inspector Tan, what CCTV evidence was reviewed?
PW2: SMRT CCTV footage from the train carriage was reviewed. The relevant
train — Train 305 on the North-South Line — was identified. The footage
shows the complainant standing at the pole. At the timestamp 21:17:42,
a man standing immediately behind the complainant makes a downward hand
movement in the direction of the complainant's lower body. The footage
is of limited resolution, but the movement is visible.

DPP ANG: Is the person shown in the CCTV identifiable?
PW2: Partially. We can confirm a dark-coloured shirt. We could not
read facial features clearly from the CCTV.

DPP ANG: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. RAJ: Inspector Tan, the CCTV timestamp shows 21:17:42. The
complainant initially stated the incident was "around 9:15pm." Is
9:17pm consistent with that?
PW2: That is broadly consistent.

MR. RAJ: The CCTV shows the train was between Ang Mo Kio and Bishan
at 21:17:42. The journey time is approximately 3 minutes. When did
the train leave Ang Mo Kio?
PW2: Train 305 departed Ang Mo Kio at 21:15:09.

MR. RAJ: My client's Stored Value Transit Card record, Exhibit D6,
shows an entry tap at Toa Payoh MRT at 21:18:44. Toa Payoh is two
stops north of Ang Mo Kio. If the train was between Ang Mo Kio and
Bishan at 21:17, the accused could not physically have entered a
train at Toa Payoh at 21:18. Is that correct?
PW2: Those two timestamps would be physically incompatible, yes.

MR. RAJ: No further questions.

DPP ANG: Your Honour, the Prosecution will need to verify the EZ-Link
card records, which were disclosed by the defence late. The prosecution
requests time to examine Exhibit D6 before closing.

COURT: This is granted. The court notes the critical contradiction:
If the accused's transit card shows an entry tap at Toa Payoh at 21:18,
and the incident took place on a train between Ang Mo Kio and Bishan
at 21:17, these two facts cannot both be true.
Additionally: PW1's description of the shirt changed from "dark blue
collared" in her statement to "dark green" to "may have been a T-shirt"
in court. The description of which buttock was touched also changed.

[HEARING ADJOURNED TO 10 MARCH 2025 FOR SMRT CARD AND CCTV VERIFICATION]

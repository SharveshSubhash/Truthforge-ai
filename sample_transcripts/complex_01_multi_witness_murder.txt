HIGH COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. HC/CC 3/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v SHAUN TAN WEI MING

Date of Hearing: 10 February 2025 (Day 4 of trial)
Coram: Justice Hoo Sheau Peng
Counsel for Prosecution: Senior Deputy Public Prosecutor Mr. Desmond Lam Keng Yeow
and DPP Ms. Clara Sng Hui Ying
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Peter Cuthbert Low SC and Ms. Rachel Ng Xin Yi

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused faces a charge under s.300(c) of the Penal Code — murder —
specifically that on 7 September 2024 between 11:30pm and 1:00am, at
Unit #08-22 of Rivervale Crescent Condominium, Sengkang, he caused
the death of Mr. Kenneth Chia Zhi Hao by inflicting multiple stab wounds.
The accused and the deceased were former business partners. This is Day 4
of trial. The hearing today covers the testimony of three witnesses.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW4 (Fourth Prosecution Witness — Ms. Amanda Lee
Shu Yi, resident of #08-20, Rivervale Crescent Condominium, direct
neighbour of the deceased)

SDPP LAM: Ms. Amanda, did you hear anything unusual on the night of
7 September 2024?
PW4: Yes. I heard very loud banging sounds — like something heavy hitting
the floor. I also heard shouting. Two male voices. The noise came
clearly through the shared wall between my unit and #08-22. I know
Kenneth — we've been neighbours for three years. One of the voices
sounded like his. The other voice I did not recognise.

SDPP LAM: What time did you hear this?
PW4: It was around midnight. I had gone to bed at 11:45pm but I hadn't
fallen asleep. The noise woke me up properly, so it was maybe 12:05am
to 12:15am. I remember looking at my bedside clock after the noise
started.

SDPP LAM: How long did the noise continue?
PW4: Maybe 10 to 15 minutes. Then it went quiet.

SDPP LAM: Did you call the police?
PW4: Not immediately. I listened for a while. When it went completely
quiet I thought maybe it was over. I went back to sleep. I only
found out the next morning when police were at the door.

SDPP LAM: Did you hear the front door of #08-22 open or close at any
point?
PW4: I heard a door — I assumed it was the front door — slam once.
That was shortly after the noise stopped. Maybe 12:20am.

SDPP LAM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW4

MR. LOW SC: Ms. Amanda, you said you looked at your bedside clock.
What type of clock is it?
PW4: A digital alarm clock. Red numbers.

MR. LOW SC: And you saw the noise started at 12:05 to 12:15am?
PW4: Approximately. I remember the clock showed something like 12:07
or 12:08. Around there.

MR. LOW SC: You also heard a door slam at around 12:20am. That is
approximately 5 to 15 minutes after the noise stopped?
PW4: Yes. The noise stopped and then a bit later I heard the door.

MR. LOW SC: Could the door slam have been from another unit on the
same floor? The eighth floor has eight units.
PW4: It came from the direction of #08-22. But I can't be completely
certain it wasn't another unit.

MR. LOW SC: You did not call the police. Does that mean you did not
consider the situation an emergency?
PW4: I thought it was a domestic argument. Arguments do happen.
I didn't realise what had actually happened.

MR. LOW SC: No further questions.

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW5 (Fifth Prosecution Witness — Mr. David Ong
Bak Chuan, Security Guard, Rivervale Crescent Condominium, on duty
at the guardhouse from 11pm to 7am on 7–8 September 2024)

SDPP LAM: Mr. David, what is your post and what are your duties?
PW5: I'm a security officer. On that night I was stationed at the main
vehicle gate. I also monitor the CCTV console which covers the main
lobby, carpark, and the access corridor on each floor.

SDPP LAM: Did you observe the accused, Mr. Shaun Tan, entering the
condominium on the night of 7 September 2024?
PW5: Yes. He came in through the main pedestrian gate at 11:34pm.
I remember because he stopped to ask me which floor Kenneth Chia
lived on. I checked the resident directory and told him — Block 8,
eighth floor. I noted it in my logbook.

SDPP LAM: Did you see him leave?
PW5: Yes. He came back down to the main lobby at 12:28am. He was
walking quickly. He had changed his shirt. When he came in, he was
wearing a dark blue collared shirt. When he left, he was in a plain
grey T-shirt. He left through the pedestrian gate.

SDPP LAM: Are you certain about the shirt change?
PW5: I am certain. I commented to myself because I thought it unusual.

SDPP LAM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW5

MR. LOW SC: Mr. David, you said the accused asked you which floor
Kenneth lived on at 11:34pm. Is that in your logbook?
PW5: Yes. Exhibit P12 is a copy of my logbook entry.

MR. LOW SC: Your logbook entry reads: "11:34pm — male visitor to Blk 8,
asked for resident name: Chia. Directed to 8th floor." It does not
record the visitor's name or description. How do you know this was
Mr. Shaun Tan?
PW5: I recognised him because his photo was shown to me when police
came the next morning. I connected him to the visitor I had seen.

MR. LOW SC: So you identified him from a police photograph the next
morning — not contemporaneously?
PW5: Yes, but I had a clear look at him for several minutes. He was
at my counter.

MR. LOW SC: The CCTV at the main lobby — was it working that night?
PW5: The camera at the main lobby was operational. The floor cameras
were working as well.

MR. LOW SC: Is the lobby camera footage in evidence?
SDPP LAM: Yes, Your Honour. Exhibit P13 — the lobby camera shows a
male individual entering at 11:34pm and leaving at 12:28am. The
image quality is sufficient to see the shirt colour change. The
prosecution will address this in closing.

MR. LOW SC: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW6 (Sixth Prosecution Witness — Dr. Valerie Tan
Hui Ling, Forensic Pathologist, Health Sciences Authority)

SDPP LAM: Dr. Valerie, you conducted the post-mortem on the deceased,
Mr. Kenneth Chia Zhi Hao. What were your findings?
PW6: The deceased sustained nine stab wounds. Three were to the chest,
four to the abdomen, and two to the upper back. The fatal wound was
a penetrating wound to the left ventricle of the heart — a stab wound
approximately 11 centimetres in depth. Death was caused by exsanguination
— blood loss — resulting from this wound. The wounds are consistent
with a single-edged blade approximately 2 to 2.5 centimetres wide.

SDPP LAM: What was the estimated time of death?
PW6: Based on body temperature, rigor mortis, and lividity, I estimate
death occurred between 11:45pm on 7 September and 1:15am on
8 September 2024. I cannot narrow it further without additional context.

SDPP LAM: Were there any defensive injuries?
PW6: Yes. There were six lacerations to the palms and fingers of both
hands — classic defensive wounds consistent with the deceased attempting
to grab or block a blade.

SDPP LAM: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW6

MR. LOW SC: Dr. Valerie, your time of death estimate is a 90-minute
window: 11:45pm to 1:15am. Is it correct that death could have
occurred as early as 11:45pm?
PW6: Yes, the lower bound of my estimate is 11:45pm.

MR. LOW SC: The accused entered the condominium at 11:34pm. If death
occurred at 11:45pm, that is only 11 minutes after arrival. Is it
plausible that a fatal altercation with nine stab wounds and significant
defensive struggle could occur and conclude within 11 minutes?
PW6: It is within the realm of possibility. Violent altercations can
be brief and intense.

MR. LOW SC: Or death could have occurred at 1:15am — 47 minutes after
the accused left at 12:28am?
PW6: Theoretically, yes. The physiology is consistent with both.

MR. LOW SC: Your estimate does not exclude the possibility that the
deceased was alive when the accused left the condominium?
PW6: I cannot exclude that possibility based on the pathology alone.

MR. LOW SC: No further questions.

---

SDPP LAM: Re-examination of PW6.
SDPP LAM: Dr. Valerie, the blood loss from a wound to the left
ventricle — in your opinion, how rapidly would incapacitation and
death follow?
PW6: A penetrating wound to the left ventricle is rapidly fatal without
immediate medical intervention. Loss of consciousness would typically
occur within 2 to 5 minutes of the wound being inflicted. Death would
follow within 5 to 15 minutes without surgical intervention. It is very
unlikely that a person with this wound could survive for 47 additional
minutes without intervention.

SDPP LAM: No further questions.

COURT: The following observations are recorded for the trial record.

CONSISTENT FACTS:
- The accused entered Rivervale Crescent Condominium at 11:34pm on 7 September 2024 (PW5 logbook, CCTV Exhibit P13)
- Disturbance sounds were heard from #08-22 at approximately 12:07–12:08am (PW4)
- The accused departed the condominium at 12:28am (PW5, CCTV)
- The accused had changed his shirt between entry and exit (PW5)

INCONSISTENCY REQUIRING RESOLUTION:
- PW4 heard the noise begin at 12:07–12:08am, approximately 33–34 minutes after the accused's arrival. But PW6's re-examination evidence suggests death from the cardiac wound would likely have occurred within 5 to 15 minutes of infliction. This creates a question as to the timing of the fatal wound relative to the onset of the disturbance.
- PW4 heard a door slam at approximately 12:20am. The CCTV and PW5 place the accused's departure at 12:28am — approximately 8 minutes later.

The question of whether the door slam at 12:20am was the accused's departure or a separate event requires resolution by the parties.

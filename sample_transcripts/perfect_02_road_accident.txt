DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 61/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v TAN ZHENG HUI

Date of Hearing: 19 March 2025
Coram: District Judge Andrew Pang Wei Heng
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Ms. Rebecca Loh Chin Ling
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Sathiyaselvam s/o Suppiah

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.338(b) of the Penal Code for causing grievous
hurt by a rash act not amounting to culpable homicide. The victim, Ms. Yeoh
Soo Fern, sustained a fractured right leg and two fractured ribs after being
struck by the accused's motorcycle at the junction of Commonwealth Avenue
West and Clementi Avenue 3 on 3 November 2024 at 7:28am.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Ms. Yeoh Soo Fern,
victim, 34 years old, civil servant)

DPP LOH: Ms. Yeoh, please describe what happened on the morning of
3 November 2024.
PW1: I was crossing Commonwealth Avenue West at the signalised pedestrian
crossing at the junction with Clementi Avenue 3. The pedestrian signal
was green — the green man was showing. I was about three-quarters of
the way across when I was hit by a motorcycle. I woke up in NUH.

DPP LOH: What time was it?
PW1: 7:28am. I was on my way to work at the MOE headquarters at
Buona Vista. I left my flat at Clementi Avenue 3 at 7:15am and it
is about a 10-minute walk. I remember checking my phone at 7:27am
to see if there were any emails.

DPP LOH: What injuries did you sustain?
PW1: A fractured right fibula, two fractured ribs on the right side,
and multiple lacerations. I was hospitalised for six days and on
medical leave for eleven weeks.

DPP LOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. SATHIYA: Ms. Yeoh, was the pedestrian light definitely on green
when you were crossing?
PW1: Yes, I checked before I stepped off the kerb. It was green.

MR. SATHIYA: Is it possible the light had changed to red by the time
you were three-quarters across?
PW1: The phase had been green for maybe 10 seconds before I started.
I was walking at a normal pace. It should have had at least another
20 seconds remaining.

MR. SATHIYA: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW2 (Second Prosecution Witness — Mr. Bernard Koh
Siak Hng, eyewitness, motorist stopped at the junction)

DPP LOH: Mr. Bernard, were you at the junction of Commonwealth Avenue West
and Clementi Avenue 3 on 3 November 2024?
PW2: Yes. I was stopped at the traffic light in the left-turning lane, waiting
to turn into Clementi Avenue 3.

DPP LOH: What did you observe?
PW2: The pedestrian crossing signal was green and a woman was crossing.
A motorcycle then came from behind me, moved into the gap at the left
of my car, and went through the red light at speed. He hit the woman
who was already two-thirds of the way across.

DPP LOH: Was the traffic light red for the accused's direction of travel?
PW2: Definitely. My light was also red. I was stopped. The cross traffic
had the green.

DPP LOH: What time was it?
PW2: 7:28am. I remember because the radio was on and the 7:30am news
was about to start — they were doing the 7:28am weather report.

DPP LOH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW2

MR. SATHIYA: Mr. Bernard, the accused's motorcycle came from behind you.
Could it have been from a different lane rather than cutting across?
PW2: No. He squeezed past on my left side and ran the red light. It was
very clear.

MR. SATHIYA: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW3 (Third Prosecution Witness — Inspector Joanne
Ang Hui Ling, Traffic Police)

DPP LOH: Inspector Ang, what did your investigations find?
PW3: Traffic camera footage from LTA Camera No. CW-093 at the junction
confirms that at 07:28:14, the pedestrian crossing signal was green
for pedestrians. The traffic signal for vehicles on Commonwealth Avenue
West had been red for 8 seconds. The accused's motorcycle, bearing licence
plate FBA 9234L, entered the junction at 07:28:16 while the light was
red and struck the victim at 07:28:18.

DPP LOH: Was the accused cooperative?
PW3: Yes. He remained at the scene, called for an ambulance, and gave a
statement. He said he had not seen the red light as he was checking his
GPS device.

DPP LOH: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Tan Zheng Hui)

MR. SATHIYA: Mr. Tan, what were you doing at the time of the accident?
DW1: I was on my way to work. I was late. I was looking at my GPS on my
phone which was mounted on the handlebar. I looked up and I had already
entered the junction. I didn't see the red light. I saw the woman a
split second before impact. There was no time to brake.

MR. SATHIYA: Did you stop after the accident?
DW1: Yes, immediately. I called 995 for an ambulance and stayed until
the ambulance and police arrived. I never tried to leave.

DPP LOH: Mr. Tan, you acknowledge you ran a red light?
DW1: Yes. I wasn't paying sufficient attention. I deeply regret what happened
to Ms. Yeoh. I take full responsibility.

DPP LOH: No further questions.

COURT: The testimony of all witnesses is internally consistent and
mutually corroborating. All witnesses and the LTA camera confirm:
(1) The pedestrian light was green at 7:28am; (2) The vehicle traffic
light was red; (3) The accused entered the junction while the light
was red; (4) The accused struck the victim who was crossing legally.
The accused himself does not contest these facts.

[CASE ADJOURNED FOR MITIGATION PLEA ON 21 MARCH 2025]

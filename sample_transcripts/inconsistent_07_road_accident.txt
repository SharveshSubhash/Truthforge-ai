DISTRICT COURT OF SINGAPORE
CRIMINAL CASE NO. DC/CC 155/2025

PUBLIC PROSECUTOR v LEE CHENG KWONG

Date of Hearing: 1 April 2025
Coram: District Judge Teo Wei Ling
Counsel for Prosecution: Deputy Public Prosecutor Mr. Poh Juang Hong
Counsel for Defence: Mr. Wee Teng Han

CASE SUMMARY:
The accused is charged under s.65(1)(b) of the Road Traffic Act for driving
without due care and attention. On 8 January 2025, the accused's van collided
with a cyclist, Mr. Heng Ah Seng, 68, at the junction of Tampines Avenue 1
and Tampines Avenue 10. The cyclist sustained a fractured collarbone and
concussion. The accused's account of the collision has evolved between his
police statement, his police interview, and his court testimony.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF PW1 (First Prosecution Witness — Mr. Heng Ah Seng,
victim, 68 years old, retired)

DPP POH: Mr. Heng, can you describe what happened?
PW1: I was cycling along Tampines Avenue 1 toward the junction. The
traffic light for cyclists was green. I was in the cycling lane. A van
came from my right, turning left across my lane. It hit me. I flew off
my bicycle. I remember hitting the ground. Next thing I remember is the
ambulance.

DPP POH: Did you see the colour of the traffic light when you entered
the junction?
PW1: It was green. I am certain.

DPP POH: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF PW1

MR. WEE: Mr. Heng, you said you are "certain" the light was green. But
you were concussed in the accident. Are you sure you accurately recall
the light colour?
PW1: I saw it before the impact. My memory is clear up to the moment of
the collision.

MR. WEE: In your police statement on 9 January 2025 — the day after the
accident — you stated: "I don't remember whether the light was green
or red when I entered the junction." How can you be certain now but not
the day after?
PW1: My memory came back. I was still concussed on the 9th. By the time
I gave the formal statement I was more settled.

MR. WEE: No further questions.

---

EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF OF DW1 (The Accused — Mr. Lee Cheng Kwong)

MR. WEE: Mr. Lee, describe what happened at the junction.
DW1: I was turning left from Tampines Avenue 10 into Tampines Avenue 1.
My light was green with a left-turn green arrow. I had checked the
junction and did not see any cyclist. I turned. The cyclist appeared
suddenly from my left, very fast. I braked but could not avoid him.
It was not my fault — he came out of nowhere.

MR. WEE: Was your indicator on?
DW1: Yes. Always.

MR. WEE: No further questions.

---

CROSS-EXAMINATION OF DW1

DPP POH: Mr. Lee, in your accident report filed on 8 January 2025 —
the day of the accident — you stated: "I was making a left turn. I did
not see the cyclist until the last moment. I may have misjudged the speed
of the cyclist." You used the words "may have misjudged." Today in court
you say it was not your fault. Which is correct?
DW1: "May have" is just an expression. I was in shock when I wrote the report.

DPP POH: In your traffic police interview on 15 January 2025, you stated:
"I think I was checking my side mirror when I turned." But today you
say you "checked the junction and did not see any cyclist." Were you
looking in the mirror or at the junction?
DW1: I checked both. I looked at the mirror and then at the junction.

DPP POH: LTA traffic camera footage, Exhibit P3, shows the cyclist was
visible in the junction approach for approximately 2.3 seconds before
impact. You say you did not see him. Does the footage show you looking
to your left before the turn?
DW1: I can't comment on what the camera shows.

DPP POH: The footage does not show a left-head-check by the driver before
the turn. Your indicator is visible in the footage, so that part of your
account is confirmed. But the head-check you describe does not appear
in the footage.
DW1: I made the check. The camera angle may not capture everything.

DPP POH: No further questions.

COURT: The court notes the following inconsistencies:
(1) PW1 stated in his day-after police statement that he could not
remember the light colour; in court he says he is certain it was green.
He attributes this to recovering memory, which the court will assess.
(2) The accused's accident report uses the words "may have misjudged"
but his court testimony asserts no fault whatsoever.
(3) The accused described checking the junction in court, but told
police on 15 January that he was checking his mirror at the time of
the turn. These are inconsistent accounts of where he was looking.
(4) The LTA footage does not appear to show a head-check, contrary to
the accused's account.
